# Rare-word replacement grammar rules (owner rulings, 2026-07-16)

When proposing a replacement for a rare word in a KJV verse (witness-batch second pass, roadmap Decision Log #12):

1. **Not one-for-one.** A rare word may be replaced by several words or a rephrased clause when that is clearer. Clarity beats mechanical substitution.
2. **Carry the surrounding function words.** A swapped word must bring its required auxiliaries, articles, and prepositions with it — check the whole phrase, not just the word:
   - **Auxiliary agrees with the new word's part of speech.** "are accustomed" (adjective) → "have learned" (past participle needs *have*). Owner-ruled example: Jeremiah 13:23 "that are accustomed to do evil" → "that have learned to do evil" (Wycliffe "han lerned", DRC "have learned").
   - **a/an agrees with the new word's initial sound.** "an adamant" → "a diamond", never "an diamond".
   - Second-person inflections must survive: "thou abhorrest" cannot become "thou abhor".
3. **The rare word itself must go — never keep it or merely re-inflect it.** Under the project premise the flagged rare word is itself the suspected alteration artifact: a word that rare would not have been the original reading. So "accuser → accusers", "abhorrest → abhor", or reverting to the flagged word (verdict KEEP-the-original) are all invalid. Choose a genuinely different word or phrase, even when the flagged word seems common in modern English (acre, adorn) — its rarity *within the KJV corpus* is what flagged it. If truly no period-authentic alternative exists, say NO-SAFE-SWAP with rationale rather than keeping the word.
4. **The replacement must not itself be KJV-rare.** Don't cure one rare word with another: prefer words well-attested in the KJV corpus (in the needed inflection). Example: for "abhorrest" (Romans 2:22), "detestest" was rejected (0 KJV occurrences — it would become a new rare word); "hatest" chosen (common: Psalm 5:5 etc., and already the audited choice for abhorrest at Isaiah 7:16). Check the exact inflected form's frequency, not just the lemma's.
5. **Never invert the verse's meaning** (owner ruling 2026-07-17). Before finalizing any NEW text, re-read it against OLD and confirm the meaning is preserved — past auto-selections silently inverted sense ("persecuting the church" → "following the church"; a healing rite → "destroying"; pursuit → retreat). If every candidate changes what the verse says, that is NO-SAFE-SWAP, not a swap.
6. **Relationship words** (owner ruling 2026-07-17): in the biblical/KJV era the only kinship words are **husband, wife, brother, sister, father, mother** — no cousins, aunts, uncles, grandmothers, grandfathers. Compound around those instead: grandmother → "mother's mother" (II Timothy 1:5), aunt → "father's sister"/"mother's sister" style phrasing. Specific rulings: "horseleach" (Proverbs 30:15) → probably "leach"; Jacob's "ladder" (Genesis 28:12) stays — do NOT change it.
7. **Verse references use "Revelation #:#"** (owner ruling 2026-07-17), not the database book name "Revelation of John" — write the shorthand in all owner-facing output (proposal headers, reports, prose). The db lookups still need the full name (see corpus-quirks).
8. **Witnesses model the construction, not just the word.** When a period witness (Wycliffe, Tyndale, Geneva, DRC) uses a construction ("have learned"), prefer copying its grammar over grafting the bare word into the old frame.
9. **Agent-noun → relative clause is a legitimate rephrase, not a forbidden re-inflection.** "shearer" (Acts 8:32) and "shedder" (Ezekiel 18:10) both had CUR turn the noun into the wrong part of speech ("dumb before his shorn", "a shed of blood"). Fix: rephrase as a relative clause using the verb form of the SAME lemma ("before him that sheareth him", "one that sheddeth blood") — allowed under rule 1 because the sentence structure genuinely changes, unlike bare re-inflection (rule 3) which keeps the same syntactic slot.
10. **Circular rare-for-rare swaps happen across unrelated verses.** Watch for a flagged word whose proposed replacement is *itself* independently flagged as a rare word elsewhere in the batch set — e.g. severity (Romans 11:22, 1 occ.) ↔ sharpness (2 Cor 13:10, 1 occ.) were cross-swapped for each other; slackness (1 occ.) ↔ slothfulness (2 occ., itself flagged at Proverbs 19:15 and Ecclesiastes 10:18) likewise. Resolve with a third, genuinely common word (e.g. "rigour", "idleness") rather than adopting either half of the pair.
11. **Numeric words can be corrupted by a wrong multiplier, not just a wrong lemma** — "sixscore" (=120) replaced with "twelve" is a tenfold error, a meaning inversion just like a wrong-sense word swap (Decision Log #12 owner rule extended to numbers). Restore the correct figure in period form ("an hundred and twenty").
12. **Check for existing words elsewhere in the verse**, not just the flagged word's immediate clause — CUR sometimes introduces a word that duplicates something already present (e.g. "idleness" next to an existing "idle soul" shares a root and should be avoided; "dreams" duplicating an opening "a dream" is a hard duplicate; "pools" next to existing "ponds" is fine, genuinely different words).
13. **Word-boundary DB queries need punctuation tolerance.** A LIKE query for `' '||text||' ' LIKE '% word %'` misses words followed by a comma/period with no trailing space normalization issue — undercounts (e.g. "sharply" appeared to be 0 hits by one method, 2 by regex `\bsharply\b`). Prefer Python regex `\bword\b` over SQL LIKE with manual space-padding for accurate frequency counts.
14. **Known CUR auto-selection failure modes** (found across batches 0112-0114, 2026-07-17) — check for these before trusting CUR as a starting point:
    - **CUR sometimes leaves the flagged word completely unchanged** despite the header claiming a swap (e.g. "writer's → scribes" at Ezekiel 9:2 and 9:3 — CUR text was byte-identical to OLD). Always diff CUR against the header's claimed replacement; don't assume the substitution actually happened.
    - **CUR sometimes collides with a word already elsewhere in the same verse**, producing an accidental duplicate or self-referential nonsense phrase: "suffer a suffer to live" (Exodus 22:18, CUR reused the verse's own governing verb "suffer" as the noun), "roar...roar" (Jeremiah 51:38, reused the verse's own earlier verb).
    - **CUR sometimes produces a theologically inverted/blasphemous reading**: "worshipper of God" → "a god of God" (John 9:31, Acts 19:35) — calling a human worshipper "a god" is a meaning inversion serious enough that "servant" (484 KJV hits) was used instead, per rule 5.
    - **A word that "sounds" period-appropriate can still be a zero-hit false positive for Axis-1 attestation** — "craftily" (0 KJV.db hits) and "veils" (0 hits, KJV/Geneva/Wycliffe all use the "vail(s)" spelling) both looked safe but weren't; always run the actual frequency check rather than trusting intuition about what sounds Elizabethan.
