Below is a research bibliography specifically aimed at **historical and archived material** that can be explored using the **Internet Archive (Wayback Machine)**. Some are still online today but have substantial archived content from earlier years.

> **Note:** Inclusion in this bibliography does **not** imply the claims are accurate. It is a research guide to historical discussions and archived resources.

## IX. Research Strategy

A useful way to expand this bibliography is to:

1. Search for one of the phrases above.
2. Open any older result.
3. If the page no longer exists, paste its URL into the Wayback Machine.
4. Follow outbound links from archived pages to discover additional blogs and forums that may also have been archived.

This "link-following" approach often uncovers clusters of related sites that are no longer well indexed by search engines.

## X. Notable Historical Observation

One recurring pattern is that many later discussions reference a relatively small set of earlier sources—especially dedicated websites like AlteredBible.com and Bible-Changes.com—while forum posts and personal blogs often build upon those earlier claims rather than presenting entirely independent research. ([SBCO -][2])

If you're interested in tracing how these ideas spread over time, a next step would be creating a **citation map** showing which blogs, forums, and authors reference one another from roughly 2015 onward.
