# Batch 0002 proposals

## adventure → durst — Deuteronomy 28:56
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR ("would not gave to set") is ungrammatical. "Durst" (dared) is a well-attested KJV modal (Job 32:6, John 21:12 "durst not ask"), and the negative "durst not" construction is the corpus's own idiom for reluctance to act, matching the sense of Geneva's "would neuer venture." Rephrased per rule 1 to drop the broken "would" auxiliary.
- NEW: The tender and delicate woman among you, which durst not set the sole of her foot upon the ground for delicateness and tenderness, her eye shall be evil toward the husband of her bosom, and toward her son, and toward her daughter,

## adventure → durst — Acts 19:31
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR ("would not gave himself") is broken. "Durst not enter" carries the same reluctance-to-risk-oneself sense as DRC's "venture himself" and is attested KJV idiom (durst not — Job 32:6, John 21:12, Exodus 3:6 "durst not behold"). "Venture" itself occurs in the KJV corpus only as the noun phrase "at a venture" (2 Kings), never as a verb, so it was rejected under rule 4.
- NEW: And certain of the chief of Asia, which were his friends, sent unto him, desiring him that he durst not enter into the theatre.

## adventured → jeoparded — Judges 9:17

- NEW: (For my father fought for you, and jeoparded his life far, and delivered you out of the hand of Midian:

## advertise → counsel — Numbers 24:14
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR ("I will away thee") is ungrammatical. "Counsel" as a transitive verb ("I counsel thee") is well attested in the KJV corpus (Revelation 3:18, 2 Chronicles 10:8) and matches Wycliffe's "yyue counsel to thee" and DRC's "give thee counsel."
- NEW: And now, behold, I go unto my people: come therefore, and I will counsel thee what this people shall do to thy people in the latter days.

## advertise → tell — Ruth 4:4
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR ("I thought to away thee") is ungrammatical. "Tell" is one of the most common verbs in the KJV corpus and fits the construction directly, matching RWebster's own resolution of this verse ("I thought to tell thee, saying...").
- NEW: And I thought to tell thee, saying, Buy it before the inhabitants, and before the elders of my people. If thou wilt redeem it, redeem it: but if thou wilt not redeem it, then tell me, that I may know: for there is none to redeem it beside thee; and I am after thee. And he said, I will redeem it.

## advocate → comforter — I John 2:1
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR already swaps to a genuinely different, well-attested KJV word ("comforter," 8x — and notably the very word John himself uses for the same underlying Greek paraklētos at John 14:16, making it an authentically Johannine choice), but the article is wrong: "an comforter" must be "a comforter."
- NEW: My little children, these things write I unto you, that ye sin not. And if any man sin, we have a comforter with the Father, Jesus Christ the righteous:

## affect → envy — Galatians 4:17

## affected → bitter — Acts 14:2
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "made their minds evil moved" is awkward — "moved" does not take the "evil" prefix naturally. Rephrased per rule 1 to "made their minds bitter against the brethren," using the common KJV word "bitter" (38x) and matching BBE's "made the minds... bitter."
- NEW: But the unbelieving Jews stirred up the Gentiles, and made their minds bitter against the brethren.

## affected → zealous — Galatians 4:18
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "zealously moved" is stilted. Dropping "affected" entirely and using the common KJV word "zealous" (8x, e.g. Titus 2:14) matches Darby's "right to be zealous... at all times" and reads as authentic KJV cadence.
- NEW: But it is good to be zealous always in a good thing, and not only when I am present with you.

## affecteth → breaketh — Lamentations 3:51
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "defileth" is semantically wrong (the eye does not defile the heart; it grieves it). Geneva reads "breaketh mine heart" for this very verse, and "breaketh" is common KJV usage for grief (Psalm 51:17, "a broken... heart").
- NEW: Mine eye breaketh mine heart because of all the daughters of my city.

## affectionately → earnestly — I Thessalonians 2:8
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR already swaps in a genuinely different, very common KJV word ("earnestly"); "earnestly desirous of you" reads naturally and needs no further change.
- NEW: So being affectionately desirous of you, we were willing to have imparted unto you, not the gospel of God only, but also our own souls, because ye were dear unto us.

## affording → yielding — Psalms 144:13
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "getting all manner of store" is semantically off (the garners do not "get" store, they hold and give it out). "Yielding" is common KJV usage for produce/store (Genesis 1:11-12, "yielding seed... yielding fruit") and preserves the participial construction.
- NEW: That our garners may be full, yielding all manner of store: that our sheep may bring forth thousands and ten thousands in our streets:

## affright → terrify — II Chronicles 32:18
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "to afraid them" is ungrammatical ("afraid" is not a verb). "Terrify" is attested in the KJV corpus (4x) and matches YLT/Darby/ACV/BSB directly for this verse, without repeating "trouble" which already appears later in the same clause.
- NEW: Then they cried with a loud voice in the Jews' speech unto the people of Jerusalem that were on the wall, to terrify them, and to trouble them; that they might take the city.

- NEW: And the people saw them departing, and many knew him, and ran on foot thither out of all cities, and outwent them, and came together unto him.

## afternoon → declined — Judges 19:8
- verdict: REVISE

- NEW: And he arose early in the morning on the fifth day to depart: and the damsel's father said, Comfort thine heart, I pray thee. And they tarried until the day declined, and they did eat both of them.

- rationale: CUR's "Opening and keeping" is semantically wrong (Paul was demonstrating from scripture, not keeping anything). - NEW: Opening and shewing, that Christ must needs have suffered, and risen again from the dead; and that this Jesus, whom I preach unto you, is Christ.
