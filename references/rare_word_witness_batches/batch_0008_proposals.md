# Rare word witness batch 0008 — proposals (entries 281–320)

## bedstead → bed — Deuteronomy 3:11
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "Bed" is a genuinely different lexeme from "bedstead" (not a re-inflection), common throughout the KJV corpus, and matches Geneva1599's and DRC's own rendering ("his bed was a bed of yron" / "his bed of iron") of this verse.
- NEW: For only Og king of Bashan remained of the remnant of giants; behold, his bed was a bed of iron; is it not in Rabbath of the children of Ammon? nine cubits was the length thereof, and four cubits the breadth of it, after the cubit of a man.

## bee → NO-SAFE-SWAP — Isaiah 7:18
- verdict: NO-SAFE-SWAP
- rationale: CUR's "the like" destroys the sense entirely and breaks the fly/bee pairing that figures Egypt and Assyria. "Bee" names a specific creature essential to that pairing; every witness retains it, and no synonym exists without inventing a different image.
- NEW: And it shall come to pass in that day, that the Lord shall hiss for the fly that is in the uttermost part of the rivers of Egypt, and for the bee that is in the land of Assyria.

## beetle → cricket — Leviticus 11:22
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "Beetle" names a specific insect in a list of edible locust-kin; ASV and ACV both render this "cricket," a genuinely different English word naming a kindred insect and independently attested in Middle English (Chaucer's "criket") as predating 1611.
- NEW: Even these of them ye may eat; the locust after his kind, and the bald locust after his kind, and the cricket after his kind, and the grasshopper after his kind.

## beggarly → poor — Galatians 4:9
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "Poor" matches YLT's own rendering of this verse exactly and is common throughout the KJV corpus.
- NEW: But now, after that ye have known God, or rather are known of God, how turn ye again to the weak and poor elements, whereunto ye desire again to be in bondage?

## beguile → deceive — Colossians 2:4
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "Deceive" matches Webster's and RWebster's own rendering exactly and is common in the KJV corpus.
- NEW: And this I say, lest any man should deceive you with enticing words.

## beguile → rob — Colossians 2:18
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "reward you of your reward" is a broken duplication. ASV's "rob you of your prize" attests a genuinely different, common KJV verb for the same wrongful deprivation, matched to the noun "reward" already standing in the OLD text.
- NEW: Let no man rob you of your reward in a voluntary humility and worshipping of angels, intruding into those things which he hath not seen, vainly puffed up by his fleshly mind,

## beguiling → deceiving — II Peter 2:14
- verdict: REVISE

- NEW: Having eyes full of adultery, and that cannot cease from sin; deceiving unstable souls: an heart they have exercised with covetous practices; cursed children:

## behemoth → NO-SAFE-SWAP — Job 40:15
- verdict: NO-SAFE-SWAP
- rationale: "Behemoth" is a transliterated Hebrew proper name for a specific creature, retained unchanged by every witness including DRC and BSB (which capitalizes it as a proper name). No synonym exists without inventing content.
- NEW: Behold now behemoth, which I made with thee; he eateth grass as an ox.

## behoved → was needful — Luke 24:46
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "thus it owed Christ to suffer" is not idiomatic English. "Needful" is common in the KJV corpus (Philippians 1:24, Titus 3:14) and several witnesses (RWebster, UKJV, ACV) independently render this "was necessary" / "was essential," confirming the underlying sense of a fitting necessity.
- NEW: And said unto them, Thus it is written, and thus it was needful for Christ to suffer, and to rise from the dead the third day:

## behoved → was needful — Hebrews 2:17
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Consistent with the Luke 24:46 ruling above; RWebster, UKJV, ACV, and BBE all render this "was necessary" / "was essential," and "needful" is the KJV-corpus-attested word for the same idea.
- NEW: Wherefore in all things it was needful for him to be made like unto his brethren, that he might be a merciful and faithful high priest in things pertaining to God, to make reconciliation for the sins of the people.

## bekah → portion — Exodus 38:26
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "A half for every man" is confusing, immediately followed by "that is, half a shekel." Geneva1599 attests "A portion for a man, that is, halfe a shekel," supplying a genuinely different, common KJV word (Deuteronomy 18:8 and elsewhere) for the fixed share owed by each man, and resolving the awkward doubled "half."
- NEW: A portion for every man, that is, half a shekel, after the shekel of the sanctuary, for every one that went to be numbered, from twenty years old and upward, for six hundred thousand and three thousand and five hundred and fifty men.

## bellows → blower — Jeremiah 6:29
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "Bellows" names a specific smithing tool, and every witness including pre-1611 Wycliffe retains it — but BBE independently renders this "the blower," a genuinely different English word for the same fire-tending device (compare the KJV's own "the smith that bloweth the coals," Isaiah 54:16), offering a workable substitute where the creature-name entries in this batch had none.
- NEW: The blower is burned, the lead is consumed of the fire; the founder melteth in vain: for the wicked are not plucked away.
