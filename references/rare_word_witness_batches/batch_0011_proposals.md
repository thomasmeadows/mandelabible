# Rare word witness batch 0011 — proposals (entries 401–440)

## bondmaid → bondwoman — Galatians 4:22
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "handmaid" weakens the free/bond contrast the verse itself makes explicit ("the other by a freewoman") — a handmaid need not be unfree. Darby, RWebster, and DRC all attest "bondwoman," a genuinely different word that preserves the bond/free legal distinction the passage's argument depends on, consistent with the Leviticus 19:20 ruling in the previous batch.
- NEW: For it is written, that Abraham had two sons, the one by a bondwoman, the other by a freewoman.

## bondmaids → bondwomen — Leviticus 25:44
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR wrongly reduced the plural "bondmaids" to singular "handmaid" in both places, breaking both number and sense. DRC attests "bondwomen," a genuinely different plural word that keeps the parallel with "bondmen" and is consistent with the Galatians 4:22 ruling above.
- NEW: Both thy bondmen, and thy bondwomen, which thou shalt have, shall be of the heathen that are round about you; of them shall ye buy bondmen and bondwomen.

## bondservant → slave — Leviticus 25:39
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's plain "servant" loses the specific point of the verse, which is to forbid reducing this brother to slave status specifically (an ordinary servant relationship would otherwise be unremarkable). RWebster attests "slave," a genuinely different word that preserves the legal force of the prohibition.
- NEW: And if thy brother that dwelleth by thee be waxen poor, and be sold unto thee; thou shalt not compel him to serve as a slave:

## bondservice → forced service — I Kings 9:21
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's bare "service" loses the compelled, unfree character of the labor. RWebster's "forced labour" and BBE's "forced work" attest the same sense; "forced service" is a genuinely different phrase preserving that sense while fitting "a tribute of ___" from the OLD text.
- NEW: Their children that were left after them in the land, whom the children of Israel also were not able utterly to destroy, upon those did Solomon levy a tribute of forced service unto this day.

## booth → lodge — Job 27:18
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "tabernacle" carries the wrong connotation — a sacred dwelling rather than the flimsy, temporary hut central to this simile about an unstable house. Geneva1599 attests "a lodge that the watchman maketh," a genuinely different word preserving the sense of a makeshift shelter.
- NEW: He buildeth his house as a moth, and as a lodge that the keeper maketh.
