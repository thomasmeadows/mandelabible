# Rare word witness batch 0012 — proposals (entries 441–480)

## bred → brought forth — Exodus 16:20
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "it high worms" is ungrammatical nonsense. "brought forth" (511 occurrences) is extremely well attested and matches the sense of YLT "bringeth up worms".
- NEW: Notwithstanding they hearkened not unto Moses; but some of them left of it until the morning, and it brought forth worms, and stank: and Moses was wroth with them.

## breed → swarm — Genesis 8:17
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "may appointed abundantly" is ungrammatical. "swarm" (42 occurrences) is well attested and matches Darby "swarm on the earth".
- NEW: Bring forth with thee every living thing that is with thee, of all flesh, both of fowl, and of cattle, and of every creeping thing that creepeth upon the earth; that they may swarm abundantly in the earth, and be fruitful, and multiply upon the earth.

## breed → fed in — Deuteronomy 32:14
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "rams of the appointed of Bashan" is nonsense. Copying Geneva1599's construction "rammes fed in Bashan" ("fed in" attested 56 times) gives a natural, well-attested reading.
- NEW: Butter of kine, and milk of sheep, with fat of lambs, and rams fed in Bashan, and goats, with the fat of kidneys of wheat; and thou didst drink the pure blood of the grape.

## breeding → possession — Zephaniah 2:9
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "growth" is a genuinely different word but rare in the KJV corpus (7 occurrences). "possession" (161 occurrences) is far better attested and matches Darby/ASV/ACV "possession of nettles" exactly.
- NEW: Therefore as I live, saith the Lord of hosts, the God of Israel, Surely Moab shall be as Sodom, and the children of Ammon as Gomorrah, even the possession of nettles, and saltpits, and a perpetual desolation: the residue of my people shall spoil them, and the remnant of my people shall possess them.

## bribe → ransom — I Samuel 12:3
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "ransom" is a genuinely different word, well attested in KJV (112 occurrences), and matches YLT "ransom".
- NEW: Behold, here I am: witness against me before the Lord, and before his anointed: whose ox have I taken? or whose ass have I taken? or whom have I defrauded? whom have I oppressed? or of whose hand have I received any ransom to blind mine eyes therewith? and I will restore it you.

## bribe → ransom — Amos 5:12
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Same as I Samuel 12:3 — "ransom" is well attested (112) and matches YLT "ransoms".
- NEW: For I know your manifold transgressions and your mighty sins: they afflict the just, they take a ransom, and they turn aside the poor in the gate from their right.

## briefly → wholly — Romans 13:9
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "throughly comprehended" reads as "thoroughly" — a poor sense-fit for "briefly." "wholly" (203 occurrences) is well attested and fits "wholly comprehended in this saying" (all commandments contained entirely in one), matching the sense behind Darby/ASV/YLT/BSB's "summed up."
- NEW: For this, Thou shalt not commit adultery, Thou shalt not kill, Thou shalt not steal, Thou shalt not bear false witness, Thou shalt not covet; and if there be any other commandment, it is wholly comprehended in this saying, namely, Thou shalt love thy neighbour as thyself.

## briefly → shortly — I Peter 5:12
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "written throughly" contradicts the verse's sense of a short letter. "shortly" (105 occurrences) is well attested and matches Wycliffe's "wroot schortli" for this very verse.
- NEW: By Silvanus, a faithful brother unto you, as I suppose, I have written shortly, exhorting, and testifying that this is the true grace of God wherein ye stand.

## brigandine → harness — Jeremiah 51:3
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "harness" (armor) is a genuinely different word, attested 42 times in KJV, and is itself KJV idiom for armor (1 Kings 20:11, "girdeth on his harness").
- NEW: Against him that bendeth let the archer bend his bow, and against him that lifteth himself up in his harness: and spare ye not her young men; destroy ye utterly all her host.

## brigandines → harness — Jeremiah 46:4
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Same reasoning as Jeremiah 51:3 — "harness" as armor is well-attested KJV idiom.
- NEW: Harness the horses; and get up, ye horsemen, and stand forth with your helmets; furbish the spears, and put on the harness.

## bringers → they that brought up — II Kings 10:5
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "the fathers up of the children" is ungrammatical. Rephrasing as "they that brought up" ("brought up" attested 511 times) matches ASV's construction closely.
- NEW: And he that was over the house, and he that was over the city, the elders also, and they that brought up the children, sent to Jehu, saying, We are thy servants, and will do all that thou shalt bid us; we will not make any king: do thou that which is good in thine eyes.

## broader → greater than — Job 11:9
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "large than the sea" is ungrammatical (needs a comparative). KJV avoids -er comparatives here; "greater than" (217 occurrences) is a common, well-attested KJV comparative construction.
- NEW: The measure thereof is longer than the earth, and greater than the sea.

## broided → broidered — I Timothy 2:9

- NEW: In like manner also, that women adorn themselves in modest apparel, with shamefacedness and sobriety; not with broidered hair, or gold, or pearls, or costly array;

## broiled → roasted — Luke 24:42
- verdict: REVISE

- NEW: And they gave him a piece of a roasted fish, and of an honeycomb.

## brokenfooted → lame — Leviticus 21:19
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "bruised, or brokenhanded" collides with the companion entry (brokenhanded→bruised) on the same verse, which would produce a duplicated "bruised...bruised." "lame" (1039 occurrences) is well attested for the foot, and "maimed" (49 occurrences) is used for the companion word to keep both entries consistent and non-duplicative.
- NEW: Or a man that is lame, or maimed,

## brokenhanded → maimed — Leviticus 21:19
- verdict: REVISE

- NEW: Or a man that is lame, or maimed,

## brotherhood → brethren — I Peter 2:17

## brow → forehead — Isaiah 48:4

## brow → edge of the hill — Luke 4:29
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "forehead of the hill" applies the literal body-part sense of "forehead" awkwardly as a metaphor for a hill's edge. Copying Tyndale and Geneva1599's construction "edge of the hill" avoids the strained metaphor while staying in period voice.
- NEW: And rose up, and thrust him out of the city, and led him unto the edge of the hill whereon their city was built, that they might cast him down headlong.

- NEW: Behold, the noise of the rumour is come, and a great commotion out of the north country, to make the cities of Judah desolate, and a den of dragons.
