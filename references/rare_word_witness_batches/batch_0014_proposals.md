# Rare word witness batch 0014 — proposals (entries 521–560)

## calved → delivered — Jeremiah 14:5
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "delivered" is genuinely different, well attested, and fits the sense of giving birth.
- NEW: Yea, the hind also delivered in the field, and forsook it, because there was no grass.

## calveth → bringeth forth — Job 21:10
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR merely modernized the flagged word's spelling (calveth→calves), the same lemma. "bringeth forth" (168 occurrences) is well attested and genuinely different.
- NEW: Their bull gendereth, and faileth not; their cow bringeth forth, and casteth not her calf.

## camphire → myrrh — Song of Solomon 1:14
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "cluster of ransom" is nonsensical (wrong semantic domain — "ransom" belongs to legal/financial verses, not a garden image). "myrrh" (112 occurrences) is well attested, already used in the very next verse of this book (Song 1:13), and preserves the fragrant-plant sense.
- NEW: My beloved is unto me as a cluster of myrrh in the vineyards of En-gedi.

## camphire → myrrh — Song of Solomon 4:13
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Same fix as Song 1:14 for consistency; CUR's "ransom" is nonsensical in this botanical list.

## cane → calamus — Isaiah 43:24
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "reed" is a genuinely different word but generic; "calamus" (21 occurrences, "sweet calamus" 7 — Exodus 30:23, Song 4:14) is the KJV's own exact term for this same aromatic plant, giving a more precise and well-attested replacement.
- NEW: Thou hast bought me no sweet calamus with money, neither hast thou filled me with the fat of thy sacrifices: but thou hast made me to serve with thy sins, thou hast wearied me with thine iniquities.

- NEW: Surely thou didst set them in slippery places: thou hast caused them to fall down into destruction.

## chalkstones → stones of lime — Isaiah 27:9
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "as lime that are beaten in sunder" loses the "stones" image entirely (lime, a substance, cannot naturally be "beaten in sunder" as pieces). Rephrasing as "stones of lime" preserves the stone-breaking image using the common KJV genitive pattern ("stones of X," e.g. Ezekiel 28:14 "stones of fire") with well-attested words "stones" and "lime."
- NEW: By this therefore shall the iniquity of Jacob be purged; and this is all the fruit to take away his sin; when he maketh all the stones of the altar as stones of lime that are beaten in sunder, the groves and images shall not stand up.
