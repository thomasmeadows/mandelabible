## citizen → inhabitant — Luke 15:15
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: All period witnesses (Wycliffe "citeseyns", Tyndale "citesyn", Geneva "citizen", DRC "citizens") offer no alternate word for this sense, so a genuine replacement outside that family is needed; "inhabitant" (1512 occurrences in the KJV corpus, common inflected form) preserves the sense of one dwelling in that country.
- NEW: And he went and joined himself to an inhabitant of that country; and he sent him into his fields to feed swine.
