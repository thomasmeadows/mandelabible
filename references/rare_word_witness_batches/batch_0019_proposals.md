# Batch 0019 — rare-word replacement proposals (second pass)

## complainers → backbiters — Jude 1:16
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "backbiters" is a genuinely different word, correctly inflected, and attested as period vocabulary in Geneva1599 (14 hits), fitting Jude's catalogue of vices.
- NEW: These are murmurers, backbiters, walking after their own lusts; and their mouth speaketh great swelling words, having men's persons in admiration because of advantage.

## composition → making — Exodus 30:32
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "tale" is nonsensical (a story, not a formula). Wycliffe reads "bi the makyng therof ye schulen not make another" — a period witness attesting "making" as the term for the recipe/formula, common in the corpus (182 hits).
- NEW: Upon man's flesh shall it not be poured, neither shall ye make any other like it, after the making of it: it is holy, and it shall be holy unto you.

## composition → making — Exodus 30:37
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Same fix as Exodus 30:32 — Wycliffe again attests "makyng" for this recipe/formula sense in the companion verse; "tale" is meaningless here.
- NEW: And as for the perfume which thou shalt make, ye shall not make to yourselves according to the making thereof: it shall be unto thee holy for the Lord.

## compound → tempered — Exodus 30:25
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR duplicates "ointment ointment," a broken text. "Compounded" is merely a re-inflection of the flagged lemma (disallowed). "Tempered" is a genuinely different word for mixing/blending, well attested as period vocabulary in Geneva1599 and Wycliffe (112 hits each), and common in the KJV corpus (56 hits).
- NEW: And thou shalt make it an oil of holy ointment, an ointment tempered after the art of the apothecary: it shall be an holy anointing oil.

## compoundeth → maketh — Exodus 30:33
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "maketh" is directly attested for this verse in both Geneva1599 ("make the like oyntment") and Wycliffe ("makith sich oile"), and is extremely common in the corpus (812 hits).
- NEW: Whosoever maketh any like it, or whosoever putteth any of it upon a stranger, shall even be cut off from his people.

## comprehend → know — Job 37:5
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "know" is directly attested for this verse in Geneva1599 ("which we know not") and is extremely common in the corpus.
- NEW: God thundereth marvellously with his voice; great things doeth he, which we cannot know.

## comprehend → know — Ephesians 3:18
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: No period witness diverges from "comprehend" here, so the nearest defensible genuinely-different word is the one already ruled sound at Job 37:5; "know" is extremely common in the corpus and the construction "able to know what is" is standard biblical idiom.
- NEW: May be able to know with all saints what is the breadth, and length, and depth, and height;

## concern → about — Acts 28:31
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR is grammatically broken ("things which about the Lord"). ACV models the needed clause rewrite by dropping "which": "teaching the things about the Lord Jesus Christ" — genuinely different from "concern" and grammatically sound.
- NEW: Preaching the kingdom of God, and teaching those things about the Lord Jesus Christ, with all confidence, no man forbidding him.

## concern → about — II Corinthians 11:30
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Same grammatical break as Acts 28:31 ("things which about"). Apply the same clause fix, dropping "which."
- NEW: If I must needs glory, I will glory of the things about mine infirmities.

## concerneth → pertaineth — Psalms 138:8
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "meaneth" changes the sense entirely (signifies vs. pertains to). "Pertaineth" is a genuinely different word carrying the same sense of "belongs to/concerns," common in the corpus (49 hits); its construction requires the preposition "unto," which is added per the grammar rule for swapped words.
- NEW: The Lord will perfect that which pertaineth unto me: thy mercy, O Lord, endureth for ever: forsake not the works of thine own hands.

## concerneth → pertaineth — Ezekiel 12:10
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Same fix as Psalms 138:8 — "meaneth" gives the wrong sense; "pertaineth unto" preserves the intended meaning with a genuinely different, well-attested word.
- NEW: Say thou unto them, Thus saith the Lord God; This burden pertaineth unto the prince in Jerusalem, and all the house of Israel that are among them.

## concision → false circumcision — Philippians 3:2
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: The batch itself flagged this as having no safe one-word swap. RWebster's own period-adjacent rendering clarifies the sense as "the false circumcision," a phrase built from "circumcision," well attested as period vocabulary in Geneva1599 (231 hits) and Tyndale (112 hits), and theologically accurate to Paul's wordplay against mere physical "cutting."
- NEW: Beware of dogs, beware of evil workers, beware of the false circumcision.

## concord → agreement — II Corinthians 6:15
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: No period witness diverges from "concord" here, but "agreement" is a genuinely different word, reasonably well attested in the corpus (42 hits), and captures the sense of harmony intended.
- NEW: And what agreement hath Christ with Belial? or what part hath he that believeth with an infidel?

## concourse → multitudes — Proverbs 1:21
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "roar" is nonsensical ("chief place of roar"). DRC reads "At the head of multitudes she crieth out" and Wycliffe "heed of cumpenyes" (head of crowds) — both period witnesses attest "multitude(s)" for this crowded public place, and it is extremely common in the corpus (161 hits for the plural).
- NEW: She crieth in the chief place of multitudes, in the openings of the gates: in the city she uttereth her words, saying,

## concourse → tumult — Acts 19:40
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "noise" is plausible but unsupported by any period witness (Tyndale, Geneva1599, and DRC all keep "concourse"). "Tumult" is a genuinely different, well-attested word (154 hits) for a disorderly gathering, and it echoes "uproar" earlier in the same verse, mirroring the verse's own near-repetition.
- NEW: For we are in danger to be called in question for this day's uproar, there being no cause whereby we may give an account of this tumult.

## condescend → make yourselves equal — Romans 12:16
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "away" is grammatically broken ("but away to men of low estate"). Tyndale and Geneva1599 both attest the clause "make yourselves equal to them of the lower sort" — a direct period-witness construction replacing "condescend" entirely.
- NEW: Be of the same mind one toward another. Mind not high things, but make yourselves equal to men of low estate. Be not wise in your own eyes.
