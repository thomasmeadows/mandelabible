# Rare word witness batch 0020 — proposals

## coney → rock badger — Leviticus 11:5
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "shaphan" is a bare transliteration, not English. Darby's witness rendering "rock-badger" gives an authentic period-flavored alternative; "badger"/"badgers" is well attested in KJV.db (98 hits, e.g. the tabernacle's "badgers' skins"), so "rock badger" is not itself a new rare coinage.
- NEW: And the rock badger, because he cheweth the cud, but divideth not the hoof; he is unclean unto you.

## coney → rock badger — Deuteronomy 14:7
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Same fix as Leviticus 11:5 for consistency — "shaphan" is not English; Darby's "rock-badger" is the period-witness alternative and "badger(s)" is common in KJV.db.
- NEW: Nevertheless these ye shall not eat of them that chew the cud, or of them that divide the cloven hoof; as the camel, and the hare, and the rock badger: for they chew the cud, but divide not the hoof; therefore they are unclean unto you.

## confection → composed — Exodus 30:35
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "a ointment" is both wrong article (needs "an") and wrong sense — this incense is not applied like an ointment. Geneva1599's own rendering of this verse reads "a perfume composed after the arte of the apothecarie," which drops the noun for a verb and resolves the article problem outright; DRC's "compounded" confirms the sense.
- NEW: And thou shalt make it a perfume, composed after the art of the apothecary, tempered together, pure and holy:

## conference → imparted — Galatians 2:6
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR reads "in add added nothing to me" — a broken duplicate. ASV's "imparted nothing to me" and Darby's "communicated nothing" both point to a clean verb replacing the noun clause; "impart" is attested in KJV (Romans 1:11, 1 Thessalonians 2:8).
- NEW: But of these who seemed to be somewhat, (whatsoever they were, it maketh no matter to me: God accepteth no man's person:) for they who seemed to be somewhat imparted nothing to me:

## confidently → boldly — Luke 22:59
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "constantly affirmed" shifts the meaning from "with confidence/certainty" to "continually," which is not the sense (a single assertion, not a repeated one). "Boldly" is the KJV's own idiom for confident, assertive speech (Acts 4:31, 9:27, 9:29, 14:3, 19:8) and is well attested; it matches the assertive tone Darby ("stoutly maintained") and BSB ("insisted") both preserve.
- NEW: And about the space of one hour after another boldly affirmed, saying, Of a truth this fellow also was with him: for he is a Galilean.

## conflict → fight — Philippians 1:30
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Geneva1599 renders this exact verse with "fight" ("Hauing the same fight"), a direct period-witness match, and "fight" is one of the most common nouns in the KJV corpus.
- NEW: Having the same fight which ye saw in me, and now hear to be in me.

## conflict → fighting — Colossians 2:1
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Geneva1599's witness for this verse specifically uses the gerund "fighting" ("what great fighting I haue for your sakes"), not the bare noun "fight" CUR chose. "Fightings" (plural) is attested in KJV.db (James 4:1, 2 Corinthians 7:5), so the singular gerund form is a safe, witness-matched adjustment.
- NEW: For I would that ye knew what great fighting I have for you, and for them at Laodicea, and for as many as have not seen my face in the flesh;

## conformable → made like — Philippians 3:10
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR reads "being made being unto his death" — a broken duplicated word. Wycliffe's witness "be maad lijk to his deeth" gives a clean, genuine alternative; "like unto" is an extremely common KJV construction.
- NEW: That I may know him, and the power of his resurrection, and the fellowship of his sufferings, being made like unto his death;

## conformed → made like to — Romans 8:29
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "fashioned to the image" merely grafts a new word into the old "conformed to" frame, which rule 5 disfavors. Geneva1599's own construction, "made like to the image," is the more faithful witness-copy and avoids the awkward "fashioned to" collocation.
- NEW: For whom he did foreknow, he also did predestinate to be made like to the image of his Son, that he might be the firstborn among many brethren.

## conformed → fashioned according to — Romans 12:2
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "fashioned to this world" keeps the old preposition from "conformed to," an ill fit for "fashioned." ASV's witness reading, "not fashioned according to this world," and Geneva's "fashion not your selues like vnto this worlde" both support "according to" as the natural preposition with "fashioned."
- NEW: And be not fashioned according to this world: but be ye transformed by the renewing of your mind, that ye may prove what is that good, and acceptable, and perfect, will of God.

## confused → tumultuous — Isaiah 9:5
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "confounded noise" shifts the sense from disorderly battle-din to being put to shame/perplexed, which is a different concept. Darby's "tumult" and ASV/ACV's "in the tumult" point to the tumult/uproar sense; "tumultuous noise" preserves the original noun-adjective shape while genuinely replacing the flagged word with the KJV's own battle-din vocabulary (tumult is common in the corpus).
- NEW: For every battle of the warrior is with tumultuous noise, and garments rolled in blood; but this shall be with burning and fuel of fire.

## confused → out of order — Acts 19:32
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "confounded" again shifts from "disorderly/in an uproar" to "ashamed/perplexed." Geneva1599's own witness for this verse reads "for the assemblie was out of order," a direct period-witness match for the correct sense.
- NEW: Some therefore cried one thing, and some another: for the assembly was out of order; and the more part knew not wherefore they were come together.

## congealed → curdled — Exodus 15:8
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "settled" loses the sense of solidifying/thickening. "Curdled" preserves that exact sense and is already an attested KJV word in the same semantic register (Job 10:10, "curdled me like cheese"), making it a genuine, well-grounded replacement rather than a new coinage.
- NEW: And with the blast of thy nostrils the waters were gathered together, the floods stood upright as an heap, and the depths were curdled in the heart of the sea.

## conies → rock badgers — Psalms 104:18
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "Shaphanes" is a bare transliteration/plural mangling, not English. Consistent with the Leviticus/Deuteronomy conies entries, Darby's "rock-badgers" is the period-witness alternative, and "badgers" is attested in KJV.db.
- NEW: The high hills are a refuge for the wild goats; and the rocks for the rock badgers.

## conies → rock badgers — Proverbs 30:26
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Same fix as Psalms 104:18 for consistency — "shaphanes" is not English; "rock badgers" is the witness-supported, attested alternative.
- NEW: The rock badgers are but a feeble folk, yet make they their houses in the rocks;

## consentedst → wast well pleased — Psalms 50:18
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "thou accept with him" breaks both tense and idiom. Darby's witness "thou didst take pleasure in him" points toward a "pleased/pleasure" construction; "well pleased" is one of the most common phrases in the KJV corpus (e.g. "in whom I am well pleased"), giving a strong, well-attested, genuinely different rendering in the correct past tense.
- NEW: When thou sawest a thief, then thou wast well pleased with him, and hast been partaker with adulterers.

## consist → stand — Colossians 1:17
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "commend" is wrong in sense (commend = praise/entrust, not "hold together/exist by"). "Stand" is a common, well-attested KJV verb used for being upheld/continuing in being, giving a genuine and idiomatic replacement for the abstract Latinate "consist."
- NEW: And he is before all things, and by him all things stand.

## consisteth → standeth — Luke 12:15
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "meaneth not in the abundance" is ungrammatical ("mean" does not take this construction). Tyndale and Geneva both render this exact clause with "standeth" ("his life stondeth not in the aboundaunce"/"his life standeth not in his riches"), a direct double period-witness match.
- NEW: And he said unto them, Take heed, and beware of covetousness: for a man's life standeth not in the abundance of the things which he possesseth.

## consorted → joined themselves to — Acts 17:4
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "joined with" is close in sense but grafts the new word loosely onto the old frame. Darby's witness gives the fuller idiomatic construction, "joined themselves to Paul and Silas," matching the common KJV reflexive-joining idiom (e.g. Numbers 25:3).
- NEW: And some of them believed, and joined themselves to Paul and Silas; and of the devout Greeks a great multitude, and of the chief women not a few.

## constant → stedfast — I Chronicles 28:7
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "be hold" is ungrammatical (hold is not an adjective here). Darby's witness "firm" points to steadfastness as the sense; "stedfast" is the KJV's own characteristic word for this exact idea (1 Corinthians 15:58, "be ye stedfast, unmoveable") and is well attested.
- NEW: Moreover I will establish his kingdom for ever, if he be stedfast to do my commandments and my judgments, as at this day.

## constellations → sky — Isaiah 13:10

- NEW: For the stars of heaven and the sky thereof shall not give their light: the sun shall be darkened in his going forth, and the moon shall not cause her light to shine.

## constrain → compel — Galatians 6:12
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Darby and ASV both render this exact verse with "compel," and "compel" is well attested throughout the KJV corpus (e.g. Matthew 5:41).
- NEW: As many as desire to make a fair shew in the flesh, they compel you to be circumcised; only lest they should suffer persecution for the cross of Christ.

## constraineth → compelleth — Job 32:18
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "keepeth" misses the sense of inward pressure/urging to speak. Geneva1599's witness reads "the spirite within me compelleth me" exactly; "compel" is a common KJV lemma (compelled, compel, compellest all occur), so the -eth inflection is a safe grammatical extension even though this exact form is otherwise rare.
- NEW: For I am full of matter, the spirit within me compelleth me.

## constraineth → compelleth — II Corinthians 5:14
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Same correction as Job 32:18 for consistency — "keepeth" misses the sense; "compelleth" (BSB: "Christ's love compels us") gives a genuine, witness-supported replacement built on the common lemma "compel."
- NEW: For the love of Christ compelleth us; because we thus judge, that if one died for all, then were all dead:

## constraint → necessity — I Peter 5:2
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Darby's witness renders this exact verse "not by necessity, but willingly," a direct match for CUR's word; "necessity" is well attested in the KJV corpus and closely parallels 2 Corinthians 9:7 ("not grudgingly, or of necessity"), a strong thematic match.
- NEW: Feed the flock of God which is among you, taking the oversight thereof, not by necessity, but willingly; not for filthy lucre, but of a ready mind;
