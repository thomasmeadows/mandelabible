# Batch 0021 — rare-word replacement proposals

## consult → devise — Psalms 62:4
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "counsel" ("They only counsel to cast him down") is grammatically strange — counsel wants a direct object, not "to cast." "Devise" is common in the Psalms for hostile scheming ("he deviseth mischief upon his bed," Ps 36:4) and takes an infinitive naturally in this idiom.
- NEW: They only devise to cast him down from his excellency: they delight in lies: they bless with their mouth, but they curse inwardly. Selah.

## consultation → counsel — Mark 15:1
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "held a counsel" genuinely replaces the rare word and mirrors Geneva1599's "helde a Councill" and Tyndale's "helde counsell" construction exactly.
- NEW: And straightway in the morning the chief priests held a counsel with the elders and scribes and the whole council, and bound Jesus, and carried him away, and delivered him to Pilate.

## consulter → counselleth with — Deuteronomy 18:11
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "a ask with familiar spirits" is broken (bare verb where a noun/clause is required). Geneva1599 supplies the exact construction: "that counselleth with spirits." Adapting it as a relative clause keeps the parallel list intact.
- NEW: Or a charmer, or one that counselleth with familiar spirits, or a wizard, or a necromancer.

## consulteth → taketh counsel — Luke 14:31
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "counselleth whether he be able" misreads counsel as advising others rather than deliberating with himself. Geneva1599's "taketh counsell, whether he be able" is the period construction for self-deliberation and fits the sentence cleanly.
- NEW: Or what king, going to make war against another king, sitteth not down first, and taketh counsel whether he be able with ten thousand to meet him that cometh against him with twenty thousand?

## consummation → end — Daniel 9:27
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "the end" is a genuine, common replacement, echoed by ASV's "the full end."
- NEW: And he shall confirm the covenant with many for one week: and in the midst of the week he shall cause the sacrifice and the oblation to cease, and for the overspreading of abominations he shall make it desolate, even until the end, and that determined shall be poured upon the desolate.

## contemn → scorn — Psalms 10:13
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "scorn" is a genuine, common KJV-register verb and the do-support ("doth...scorn") is grammatically correct as bare infinitive after "doth."

## contemn → scorn — Ezekiel 21:13

## contemneth → despiseth — Ezekiel 21:10

## contradiction → gainsaying — Hebrews 12:3
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "strife" is awkward here ("such strife of sinners against himself" implies mutual conflict rather than one-sided hostility endured). ASV's "such gainsaying of sinners against himself" and YLT's "such gainsaying from the sinners" match the sentence exactly and use the same word-family already approved for Acts 13:45 above.
- NEW: For consider him that endured such gainsaying of sinners against himself, lest ye be wearied and faint in your minds.

## contribution → distribution — Romans 15:26
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "fellowship" makes for strange idiom ("make a certain fellowship for the poor saints" — fellowship is a state, not a thing "made for" someone). Tyndale and Geneva1599 both read "make a certaine distribution vnto/vpon the poore Saintes," a direct period-attested construction.
- NEW: For it hath pleased them of Macedonia and Achaia to make a certain distribution for the poor saints which are at Jerusalem.

## conveniently → occasion — Mark 14:11
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: The auto-system flagged this itself as having no safe one-word swap ("season" produces the nonsensical "he sought how he might season betray him"). Rephrasing with the KJV idiom "sought occasion" (cf. - NEW: And when they heard it, they were glad, and promised to give him money. And he sought occasion how he might betray him.

- NEW: There was not a word of all that Moses commanded, which Joshua read not before all the congregation of Israel, with the women, and the little ones, and the strangers that dwelt among them.

## convert → turn him back — James 5:19

## converteth → turneth back — James 5:20

- NEW: Let him know, that he which turneth back the sinner from the error of his way shall save a soul from death, and shall hide a multitude of sins.

## converts → they that return in her — Isaiah 1:27

## convey → carry — I Kings 5:9
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "I will through them by sea" uses "through" as a verb, which is not English. "Carry" is a common, plain KJV verb that fits the sense (cf. BSB "float them as rafts"); using "float" instead would awkwardly double the following noun "floats."
- NEW: My servants shall bring them down from Lebanon unto the sea: and I will carry them by sea in floats unto the place that thou shalt appoint me, and will cause them to be discharged there, and thou shalt receive them: and thou shalt accomplish my desire, in giving food for my household.

- NEW: Holding fast the faithful word as he hath been taught, that he may be able by sound doctrine both to exhort and to confound the gainsayers.

- NEW: To execute judgment upon all, and to rebuke all that are ungodly among them of all their ungodly deeds which they have ungodly committed, and of all their hard speeches which ungodly sinners have spoken against him.

## cool → wind — Genesis 3:8
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "spirit of the day" abandons the physical sense entirely (a breeze, not a metaphysical spirit). YLT and BSB both render this exact phrase "the breeze of the day," and BBE has "the evening wind." "Wind" is common throughout the KJV corpus and captures the same physical referent as "breeze" without introducing a word absent from the corpus.
- NEW: And they heard the voice of the Lord God walking in the garden in the wind of the day: and Adam and his wife hid themselves from the presence of the Lord God amongst the trees of the garden.

## cool → refresh — Luke 16:24
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "spirit my tongue" is nonsensical (spirit cannot function as a verb here). No period witness truly supplies a different word (Wycliffe "kele" and Tyndale "cole" are simply older spellings of "cool," not distinct words). "Refresh" is common and period-attested for relief from distress (cf. Philemon 1:7, "the bowels of the saints are refreshed") and fits the torment context of this verse.
- NEW: And he cried and said, Father Abraham, have mercy on me, and send Lazarus, that he may dip the tip of his finger in water, and refresh my tongue; for I am tormented in this flame.
