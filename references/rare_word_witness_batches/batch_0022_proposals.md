# Rare word witness batch 0022 — proposals (entries 841–880)

## coping → top — I Kings 7:9
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "top" is a genuinely different, very common KJV word (637 occurrences) and matches DRC's "top of the walls" for this verse; the phrase reads as authentic 1611 construction.
- NEW: All these were of costly stones, according to the measures of hewed stones, sawed with saws, within and without, even from the foundation unto the top, and so on the outside toward the great court.

## copper → brass — Ezra 8:27
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "brasen" is an adjective with no noun to modify ("vessels of fine brasen" is ungrammatical). Geneva1599 and DRC both read "vessels of shining brass"; "brass" is a common KJV noun (882 occurrences).
- NEW: Also twenty basons of gold, of a thousand drams; and two vessels of fine brass, precious as gold.

## coppersmith → smith — II Timothy 4:14
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "founder" is ambiguous (implies "one who founds/establishes," not a metalworker). Darby renders this word "the smith"; "smith" occurs 21x in KJV.db, a common attested noun.
- NEW: Alexander the smith did me much evil: the Lord reward him according to his works:

## cor → cor — Ezekiel 45:14
- verdict: NO-SAFE-SWAP
- rationale: "cor" names a specific dry-measure unit; every witness either keeps "cor" (Geneva1599, Webster, RWebster, YLT, Darby) or transliterates it near-identically (DRC "core"). CUR's "measure" destroys the verse's technical sense (a "measure...which is an homer of ten baths" becomes circular). No witness offers a genuine common substitute, so the original technical term is retained.
- NEW: Concerning the ordinance of oil, the bath of oil, ye shall offer the tenth part of a bath out of the cor, which is an homer of ten baths; for ten baths are an homer:

## coral → coral — Job 28:18
- verdict: NO-SAFE-SWAP
- rationale: Every witness that translates this word at all (Geneva1599, Webster, RWebster, YLT "Corals", Darby "Corals", ASV, UKJV, BSB) keeps "coral" itself; none offers a distinct alternative. CUR was already flagged by the prior pass as having no safe swap; that finding stands.
- NEW: No mention shall be made of coral, or of pearls: for the price of wisdom is above rubies.

## coral → coral — Ezekiel 27:16
- verdict: NO-SAFE-SWAP
- rationale: Same word, same evidence as Job 28:18 above — Geneva1599, Webster, RWebster, YLT, and most witnesses keep "coral"; no genuine alternative is attested.
- NEW: Syria was thy merchant by reason of the multitude of the wares of thy making: they occupied in thy fairs with emeralds, purple, and broidered work, and fine linen, and coral, and agate.

## coriander → coriander — Exodus 16:31
- verdict: NO-SAFE-SWAP
- rationale: CUR's "like like seed" is a broken duplicate substitution. Every witness (Wycliffe, Geneva1599, DRC, ASV, BSB) keeps "coriander seed" as a specific plant-name with no attested alternative; restoring the original reading is the only sound option.
- NEW: And the house of Israel called the name thereof Manna: and it was like coriander seed, white; and the taste of it was like wafers made with honey.

## coriander → coriander — Numbers 11:7
- verdict: NO-SAFE-SWAP
- rationale: Same broken duplicate ("as like seed") and same evidence as Exodus 16:31 — no witness offers a substitute plant name for coriander.
- NEW: And the manna was as coriander seed, and the colour thereof as the colour of bdellium.

## cornfloor → threshingfloor — Hosea 9:1
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "every corn" is missing its noun and is ungrammatical. RWebster renders this same verse "threshingfloor," a well-attested compound in KJV.db (119 occurrences).
- NEW: Rejoice not, O Israel, for joy, as other people: for thou hast gone a whoring from thy God, thou hast loved a reward upon every threshingfloor.

## corpse → body — Mark 6:29
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Wycliffe, Tyndale, Geneva1599, and DRC all render this verse "took up his body." "body" is extremely common in KJV.db (1225 occurrences) and avoids "carcase," which in KJV usage reads as undignified for a disciple's burial of John the Baptist.
- NEW: And when his disciples heard of it, they came and took up his body, and laid it in a tomb.

## corrupters → destroyers — Isaiah 1:4
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "spoilers" shifts the sense toward military plunderers rather than moral corrupters. "corrupters" itself is being flagged elsewhere in this batch (Jeremiah 6:28) alongside "corruptly," so a replacement built on "corrupt-" risks curing one rare word with another; "destroyers" (28 occurrences in KJV.db) captures the same Hebrew root sense (mashchith, "one who ruins") and is used for the same word at Jeremiah 6:28 (see below), keeping the two parallel renderings consistent.
- NEW: Ah sinful nation, a people laden with iniquity, a seed of evildoers, children that are destroyers: they have forsaken the Lord, they have provoked the Holy One of Israel unto anger, they are gone away backward.

## corrupters → destroyers — Jeremiah 6:28
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "spoilers" is not attested by any witness for this verse. Geneva1599 reads "they all are destroyers" directly; "destroyers" is attested in KJV.db (28 occurrences).
- NEW: They are all grievous revolters, walking with slanders: they are brass and iron; they are all destroyers.

## corruptly → transgress — II Chronicles 27:2
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "did yet utterly" is ungrammatical (utterly is an adverb lacking a verb to modify). DRC reads "the people still transgressed" and BBE "the people still went on in their evil ways"; "transgress" is common in KJV.db (98 occurrences) and slots into the existing "did yet ___" construction.
- NEW: And he did that which was right in the sight of the Lord, according to all that his father Uzziah did: howbeit he entered not into the temple of the Lord. And the people did yet transgress.

## corruptly → perversely — Nehemiah 1:7
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "very utterly" is ungrammatical (double adverb with no complement). Darby renders this verse "acted very perversely against thee"; "perversely" is attested in KJV.db (21 occurrences) and fits the existing "dealt very ___" construction exactly.
- NEW: We have dealt very perversely against thee, and have not kept the commandments, nor the statutes, nor the judgments, which thou commandedst thy servant Moses.

## costliness → riches — Revelation of John 18:19
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "riches" is common in KJV.db (686 occurrences), genuinely different from "costliness," and generally consonant with RWebster's "by her wealth" and Webster's "by reason of her opulence" for this verse.
- NEW: And they cast dust on their heads, and cried, weeping and wailing, saying, Alas, alas, that great city, wherein were made rich all that had ships in the sea by reason of her riches! for in one hour is she made desolate.

## cotes → folds — II Chronicles 32:28
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "herds for flocks" is nonsensical (herds does not pair with "for flocks"; YLT's "herds" was for a different slot, "for stalls"). DRC reads "folds for cattle" and ASV "flocks in folds"; "folds" is attested in KJV.db (35 occurrences) and gives sound sense: enclosures for flocks.
- NEW: Storehouses also for the increase of corn, and wine, and oil; and stalls for all manner of beasts, and folds for flocks.

## cottage → booth — Isaiah 1:8
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "tabernacle" carries the heavily loaded sense of the sacred Tent of Meeting elsewhere in KJV, ill-suited to a garden hut. Five witnesses (Webster, RWebster, YLT, Darby, ASV, ACV) all render this word "booth"; "booth" is attested in KJV.db (14 occurrences, e.g. the Feast of Booths context) and is a genuinely different word.
- NEW: And the daughter of Zion is left as a booth in a vineyard, as a lodge in a garden of cucumbers, as a besieged city.

## cottage → lodge — Isaiah 24:20
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Matches YLT exactly ("moved as a lodge"); "lodge" is common in KJV.db (189 occurrences) and genuinely different from "cottage."
- NEW: The earth shall reel to and fro like a drunkard, and shall be removed like a lodge; and the transgression thereof shall be heavy upon it; and it shall fall, and not rise again.

## cottages → tents — Zephaniah 2:6
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: No witness supplies a direct alternative (Darby uniquely reads "cave-dwellings"; most keep "cottages"), but "tents" is a common, plausible dwelling-word for shepherds (very frequent in KJV.db) and is genuinely different from "cottages."
- NEW: And the sea coast shall be dwellings and tents for shepherds, and folds for flocks.

## couchingplace → sheepcote — Ezekiel 25:5
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "a lie for flocks" is nonsensical (reads as falsehood, not a resting-place). Geneva1599 reads "a dwelling place for camels...and the Ammonites a sheepecote"; "sheepcote" is attested in KJV.db (14 occurrences, e.g. I Samuel 24:3) and gives a concrete, sound sense.
- NEW: And I will make Rabbah a stable for camels, and the Ammonites a sheepcote for flocks: and ye shall know that I am the Lord.

## coulter → hook — I Samuel 13:20
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "plowshares" duplicates the tool already named as "share" earlier in the same verse (share and plowshare being the same implement). Geneva1599's tool-list for this verse includes "weeding hooke"; "hook" is attested in KJV.db (35 occurrences) and names a genuinely distinct farm implement.
- NEW: But all the Israelites went down to the Philistines, to sharpen every man his share, and his hook, and his axe, and his mattock.

## coulters → hooks — I Samuel 13:21
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: For consistency with the parallel verse above (coulter → hook), and because "hooks" (126 occurrences in KJV.db) is a well-attested, genuinely distinct farm-implement word not otherwise used in this verse's list (mattocks, forks, axes, goads).
- NEW: Yet they had a file for the mattocks, and for the hooks, and for the forks, and for the axes, and to sharpen the goads.
