# Rare word witness batch 0023 — proposals (entries 881–920)

## countervail → recompense — Esther 7:4
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "could not laid the king's damage" is an ungrammatical verb form. Geneva1599 reads this exact verse "the aduersarie could not recompense the Kings losse"; "recompense" is common in KJV.db (182 occurrences).
- NEW: For we are sold, I and my people, to be destroyed, to be slain, and to perish. But if we had been sold for bondmen and bondwomen, I had held my tongue, although the enemy could not recompense the king's damage.

## countrymen → nation — II Corinthians 11:26
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "mine own kind" loses the specific sense of fellow-countrymen. Geneva1599 and DRC both read "of mine own nation" / "from my own nation" for this verse; "nation" is extremely common in KJV.db (1015 occurrences).
- NEW: In journeyings often, in perils of waters, in perils of robbers, in perils by mine own nation, in perils by the heathen, in perils in the city, in perils in the wilderness, in perils in the sea, in perils among false brethren;

## countrymen → nation — I Thessalonians 2:14
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Same word as II Corinthians 11:26 above; for consistency and the same reasoning, "nation" (1015 occurrences in KJV.db) replaces "countrymen," which CUR wrongly rendered as the vague "kind."
- NEW: For ye, brethren, became followers of the churches of God which in Judea are in Christ Jesus: for ye also have suffered like things of your own nation, even as they have of the Jews:

## coveredst → deckedst — Psalms 104:6
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "cover" is a trivial re-inflection of the same flagged word (coveredst → cover), which the owner ruling forbids. "deckedst" is attested in KJV.db (Ezekiel 16:16, "deckedst thy high places with divers colours") in the identical "deckedst [object] with [garment-like thing]" construction, and is a genuinely different verb (to deck/adorn) conveying the same sense of being wrapped/arrayed.
- NEW: Thou deckedst it with the deep as with a garment: the waters stood above the mountains.

## coveredst → deckedst — Ezekiel 16:18

## crane → crane — Isaiah 38:14
- verdict: NO-SAFE-SWAP
- rationale: CUR "horse" is a broken substitution (a horse cannot "chatter" like a bird, and does not fit the migratory-bird imagery alongside the swallow and dove). Every witness that translates this verse keeps a bird — nearly all keep "crane" itself (Geneva1599, Webster, RWebster, YLT, UKJV); only BSB substitutes "thrush," an isolated, unattested choice. No genuine common alternative for this specific bird-name exists among the witnesses.
- NEW: Like a crane or a swallow, so did I chatter: I did mourn as a dove: mine eyes fail with looking upward: O Lord, I am oppressed; undertake for me.

## crane → crane — Jeremiah 8:7
- verdict: NO-SAFE-SWAP

- NEW: Yea, the stork in the heaven knoweth her appointed times; and the turtle and the crane and the swallow observe the time of their coming; but my people know not the judgment of the Lord.

## crime → wickedness — Job 31:11
- verdict: REVISE

Darby's "infamy" is unattested in KJV.db (14 occurrences, borderline) while YLT's "a wicked thing" points to the common root; "wickedness" is extremely common in KJV.db (889 occurrences) and avoids duplicating "iniquity," which already appears later in the same verse.
- NEW: For this is an heinous wickedness; yea, it is an iniquity to be punished by the judges.
