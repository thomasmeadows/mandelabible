# Rare word witness batch 0024 — proposals (entries 921–960)

## crime → charge — Acts 25:16
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Matches DRC, RWebster, YLT, and Darby, all of whom render this word "charge"; common in KJV.db (714 occurrences) and genuinely different from "crime."
- NEW: To whom I answered, It is not the manner of the Romans to deliver any man to die, before that he which is accused have the accusers face to face, and have licence to answer for himself concerning the charge laid against him.

## crimes → judgments — Ezekiel 7:23
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Matches YLT exactly ("bloody judgments") and echoes Geneva1599's "iudgement of blood"; genuinely different from "crimes" and common in KJV.db.
- NEW: Make a chain: for the land is full of bloody judgments, and the city is full of violence.

## crimes → charges — Acts 25:27
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "ordinances" wrongly names a body of law rather than an accusation. RWebster, YLT, and Darby all render this word "charges"; "charges" is attested in KJV.db (42 occurrences) and consistent with the same word's rendering at Acts 25:16 above.
- NEW: For it seemeth to me unreasonable to send a prisoner, and not withal to signify the charges laid against him.

## cripple → lame — Acts 14:8
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "being a lame from his mother's womb" is ungrammatical — "lame" is an adjective and cannot take the indefinite article "a" in this slot. YLT and Darby both drop the article ("being lame from the womb"/"lame from his mother's womb"); "lame" is common in KJV.db (189 occurrences).
- NEW: And there sat a certain man at Lystra, impotent in his feet, being lame from his mother's womb, who never had walked:

## crisping → purses — Isaiah 3:22
- verdict: REVISE

- NEW: The changeable suits of apparel, and the mantles, and the wimples, and the purses,

## crookbackt → bowed together — Leviticus 21:20
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "crookbacked" is a trivial modernized respelling of the same flagged word, not a genuinely different word. No witness offers a common attested alternative ("hunchbacked," "hump-backed," "crook-backed" are all unattested in KJV.db). The KJV itself uses "bowed together" for the identical bent-back condition at Luke 13:11 ("a woman which... was bowed together, and could in no wise lift up herself"); "bowed together" is attested in KJV.db and gives a genuinely different, period-authentic construction.
- NEW: Or bowed together, or a dwarf, or that hath a blemish in his eye, or be scurvy, or scabbed, or hath his stones broken;

- NEW: And he shall pluck away his maw with his feathers, and cast it beside the altar on the east part, by the place of the ashes:

## cumi → cumi — Mark 5:41
- verdict: NO-SAFE-SWAP
- rationale: "Talitha cumi" is an untranslated Aramaic phrase that the verse itself immediately glosses ("which is, being interpreted..."); every witness preserves the transliteration itself (varying only in spelling: "cumi"/"cumy"/"koumi"). It is not an English word to be swapped but a quoted foreign phrase.
- NEW: And he took the damsel by the hand, and said unto her, Talitha cumi; which is, being interpreted, Damsel, I say unto thee, arise.
