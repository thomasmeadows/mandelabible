# Rare word witness batch 0047 — proposals

## graveclothes → linen — John 11:44
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "linen" is genuinely different from "graveclothes", common in the KJV corpus, and matches BBE "linen bands" / BSB "strips of linen". Construction unchanged and grammatical.
- NEW: And he that was dead came forth, bound hand and foot with linen: and his face was bound about with a napkin. Jesus saith unto them, Loose him, and let him go.

## grease → gross — Psalms 119:70
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "as fat as fat" is a nonsensical duplication. Webster reads "as gross as fat"; "gross" is genuinely different from "grease", attested in KJV.db (Matthew 13:15 "this people's heart is waxed gross"), and preserves the "as X as fat" simile structure.
- NEW: Their heart is as gross as fat; but I delight in thy law.

## greaves → armour — I Samuel 17:6
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: King James agent found no one-word swap, but "armour" is genuinely different from "greaves", well attested in KJV.db (41x, including armor-of-brass contexts), and matches the general sense of Darby/BSB "armor of bronze on his legs" without inventing an unattested word like "boots" (0 KJV.db hits).
- NEW: And he had armour of brass upon his legs, and a target of brass between his shoulders.

## grievousness → sorrow of war — Isaiah 21:15
- verdict: NO-SAFE-SWAP
- rationale: Every witness (Wycliffe, Geneva, DRC, Darby, ASV, UKJV, ACV) keeps "grievousness of war"/"grievous battle" — none support "perverseness" here (that swap was mechanically carried over from the unrelated Isaiah 10:1 entry and does not fit "of war"). No period-authentic single-word substitute for "grievousness" in this sense is attested in the local witnesses that is also common in KJV.db; BSB's "stress of battle" and "sorrow of war" are both unattested constructions in the period witnesses. Recommend owner review rather than inventing an unsupported reading.
- NEW: (no safe swap identified — CUR unchanged) For they fled from the swords, from the drawn sword, and from the bent bow, and from the grievousness of war.

## growth → rain — Amos 7:1
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "latter latter" is a broken duplication. Both Wycliffe ("euentid reyn") and DRC ("latter rain") render the underlying phrase as "latter rain" (the Vulgate/Wycliffe tradition read the term as the season's rain rather than the vegetation); "rain" is genuinely different from "growth" and well attested in KJV.db (153x). This is a documented Axis-1 alternate reading, not an invention.
- NEW: Thus hath the Lord God shewed unto me; and, behold, he formed grasshoppers in the beginning of the shooting up of the latter rain; and, lo, it was the latter rain after the king's mowings.
