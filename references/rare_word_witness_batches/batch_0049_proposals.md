# Rare word witness batch 0049 — proposals

## harvestman → reaper — Isaiah 17:5
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "the reap gathereth" is ungrammatical (wrong part of speech). RWebster, Darby, and YLT all read "reaper" for this exact verse; "reaper" is genuinely different from "harvestman" and, though attested only 1x in KJV.db as this precise form, is directly supported by three independent witnesses.
- NEW: And it shall be as when the reaper gathereth the corn, and reapeth the ears with his arm; and it shall be as he that gathereth ears in the valley of Rephaim.

## harvestman → reaper — Jeremiah 9:22
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "handful after the reap" is ungrammatical. YLT and Darby both read "reaper" for this verse; kept consistent with the Isaiah 17:5 revision above.
- NEW: Speak, Thus saith the Lord, Even the carcases of men shall fall as dung upon the open field, and as the handful after the reaper, and none shall gather them.

## hatch → bring forth her young — Isaiah 34:15
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "lay, and broken, and gather" is ungrammatical. Per rule 1 (clause rewrite permitted), replacing "hatch" with "bring forth her young" is genuinely different, and "bring forth" is an extremely common KJV phrase (used throughout Genesis and elsewhere for animal and human birth).
- NEW: There shall the great owl make her nest, and lay, and bring forth her young, and gather under her shadow: there shall the vultures also be gathered, every one with her mate.

## hatch → break — Isaiah 59:5
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "They broken cockatrice' eggs" is ungrammatical (wrong verb form). DRC reads "They have broken the eggs of asps"; "break" is genuinely different from "hatch", extremely common in KJV.db, and directly matches this period witness.
- NEW: They break cockatrice' eggs, and weave the spider's web: he that eateth of their eggs dieth, and that which is crushed breaketh out into a viper.

## hatcheth → which she hath not laid — Jeremiah 17:11
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "sitteth on eggs, and travaileth them not" is an awkward transitive use of an intransitive verb. Darby and ASV both rephrase as "sitteth on eggs it/she hath not laid" (matching the Hebrew sense more directly than the traditional "hatcheth them not"); this clause rewrite (rule 1) is genuinely different from "hatcheth" and uses only common KJV vocabulary.
- NEW: As the partridge sitteth on eggs which she hath not laid; so he that getteth riches, and not by right, shall leave them in the midst of his days, and at his end shall be a fool.

## healer → helper — Isaiah 3:7
