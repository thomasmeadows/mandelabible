# Rare word witness batch 0050 — proposals

## helm → rudder — James 3:4
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: King James agent found no safe swap, but Geneva1599, Darby, ASV, and BSB all read "rudder" for this verse — the modern nautical term for the same steering device; "rudder" is genuinely different from "helm" and, though attested only 1x in KJV.db, is directly supported by four independent witnesses.
- NEW: Behold also the ships, which though they be so great, and are driven of fierce winds, yet are they turned about with a very small rudder, whithersoever the governor listeth.

## helve → handle — Deuteronomy 19:5
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "slippeth from the wood" is confusing, since "wood" (the forest) is already used earlier in the same verse. "handle" is genuinely different from "helve", was already established as a KJV.db-attested replacement word in this project (used for "haft" in batch 0048), and matches UKJV's own paraphrase "wooden handle."
- NEW: As when a man goeth into the wood with his neighbour to hew wood, and his hand fetcheth a stroke with the axe to cut down the tree, and the head slippeth from the handle, and lighteth upon his neighbour, that he die; he shall flee unto one of those cities, and live:

## herdman → herdman — Amos 7:14
- verdict: NO-SAFE-SWAP
- rationale: CUR "husbandman" materially misrepresents the text — Amos identifies himself as a herdsman (a keeper of livestock), not a husbandman (a tiller of soil); these are different occupations in the underlying Hebrew and every witness (Wycliffe, Geneva1599, DRC, RWebster, Darby, ASV, UKJV, ACV, BBE) retains "herdman"/"herdsman." No witness offers a genuinely different word preserving the correct sense, and "herdman" is itself already attested in KJV.db (1x this verse; "herdmen" plural 5x in Genesis 13), so it is not being newly invented — only its specific singular inflection is rare. Recommend owner review rather than a swap that changes Amos's stated occupation.
- NEW: (no safe swap identified — CUR unchanged, reading "herdman") Then answered Amos, and said to Amaziah, I was no prophet, neither was I a prophet's son; but I was an herdman, and a gatherer of sycomore fruit:
