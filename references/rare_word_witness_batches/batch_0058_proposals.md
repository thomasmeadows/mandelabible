# Rare word witness batch 0058 — proposals

## licence → place — Acts 25:16
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "place to answer" matches Wycliffe ("take place of defending") and Geneva ("haue place to defend himselfe") almost verbatim; sound grammar and attestation.
- NEW: To whom I answered, It is not the manner of the Romans to deliver any man to die, before that he which is accused have the accusers face to face, and have place to answer for himself concerning the crime laid against him.

## lid → door — II Kings 12:9
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "the door of it" wrongly reuses the word "door," which the same verse already uses for the temple door, creating confusion. DRC's "the top" is well-attested (5 hits) and unambiguous.
- NEW: But Jehoiada the priest took a chest, and bored a hole in the top of it, and set it beside the altar, on the right side as one cometh into the house of the Lord: and the priests that kept the door put therein all the money that was brought into the house of the Lord.

## lifter → high — Psalms 3:3
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "the high up of mine head" is ungrammatical nonsense. Rephrasing with "he that lifteth up mine head" (7 KJV.db hits for "lifteth up") preserves the sense and is grammatically sound, per the memory's rule that a rare word may be replaced by a rephrased clause.
- NEW: But thou, O Lord, art a shield for me; my glory, and he that lifteth up mine head.

## lign → tree — Numbers 24:6
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "trees of tree aloes" repeats "tree" awkwardly. Geneva's simpler "the aloe trees" avoids the redundancy while keeping the same sense; a rephrase rather than one-for-one swap.
- NEW: As the valleys are they spread forth, as gardens by the river's side, as the aloe trees which the Lord hath planted, and as cedar trees beside the waters.

## lime → plaister — Isaiah 33:12
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "plaister" (7 hits) is a genuinely distinct, period-authentic word (from "plaster," a lime-based substance) and preserves the sense of total consumption by fire.
- NEW: And the people shall be as the burnings of plaister: as thorns cut up shall they be burned in the fire.

## lime → plaister — Amos 2:1
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Same reasoning as Isaiah 33:12 — "plaister" is attested and preserves sense.
- NEW: Thus saith the Lord; For three transgressions of Moab, and for four, I will not turn away the punishment thereof; because he burned the bones of the king of Edom into plaister:

## limited → restrained — Psalms 78:41
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "restrained" wrongly implies they held God back; the Hebrew sense (per ASV "provoked," ACV "provoked") is that they tested/challenged God's patience. "provoked" is well-attested (35 hits).
- NEW: Yea, they turned back and tempted God, and provoked the Holy One of Israel.

## lineage → family — Luke 2:4
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "family" is overwhelmingly attested across witnesses (Wycliffe, DRC, YLT, Darby, ASV, BBE all read "family") and common in KJV.db.
- NEW: And Joseph also went up from Galilee, out of the city of Nazareth, into Judea, unto the city of David, which is called Bethlehem; (because he was of the house and family of David:)

## lingered → tarried — Genesis 19:16
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "tarried" (34 hits) is well-attested and echoes Geneva/Tyndale's "tarying."
- NEW: And while he tarried, the men laid hold upon his hand, and upon the hand of his wife, and upon the hand of his two daughters; the Lord being merciful unto him: and they brought him forth, and set him without the city.

## lingered → tarried — Genesis 43:10
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Same reasoning as Genesis 19:16.
- NEW: For except we had tarried, surely now we had returned this second time.

## lionlike → men — II Samuel 23:20
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "two men men of Moab" duplicates "men" — a broken text. Wycliffe, DRC, and Darby all render this "two lions of Moab" (47 hits for "lions"), naming the champions by the same heroic-lion image the flagged word alluded to.
- NEW: And Benaiah the son of Jehoiada, the son of a valiant man, of Kabzeel, who had done many acts, he slew two lions of Moab: he went down also and slew a lion in the midst of a pit in time of snow:

## lionlike → men — I Chronicles 11:22
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Same fix as II Samuel 23:20 — CUR's "men men" is a duplication error; "two lions of Moab" matches Wycliffe, DRC, and Darby.
- NEW: Benaiah the son of Jehoiada, the son of a valiant man of Kabzeel, who had done many acts; he slew two lions of Moab: also he went down and slew a lion in a pit in a snowy day.

## liquor → juice — Numbers 6:3
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "juice" is independently attested in KJV.db at Song of Solomon 8:2 ("the juice of my pomegranate"), and matches ASV/YLT/BSB directly.
- NEW: He shall separate himself from wine and strong drink, and shall drink no vinegar of wine, or vinegar of strong drink, neither shall he drink any juice of grapes, nor eat moist grapes, or dried.

## liquor → juice — Song of Solomon 7:2
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "juice" echoes the Song's own voice at 8:2 ("the juice of my pomegranate") within the same book — the strongest possible internal-KJV attestation for this erotic-imagery register.
- NEW: Thy navel is like a round goblet, which wanteth not juice: thy belly is like an heap of wheat set about with lilies.

## liquors → juices — Exodus 22:29
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "juices" is wholly unattested in KJV.db (0 hits) — curing one rare word with an equally rare one. ASV's rephrase, "the outflow of thy presses," is a period-plausible construction naming the same referent (wine/oil from the press) without inventing an unattested word-form.
- NEW: Thou shalt not delay to offer the first of thy ripe fruits, and of the outflow of thy presses: the firstborn of thy sons shalt thou give unto me.

- NEW: But I say unto you, That Elias is come already, and they knew him not, but have done unto him whatsoever they pleased. Likewise shall also the Son of man suffer of them.

## listed → pleased — Mark 9:13

- NEW: But I say unto you, That Elias is indeed come, and they have done unto him whatsoever they pleased, as it is written of him.

## listeth → desireth — John 3:8
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "listeth" means "wills, pleases" (capriciously), not "desires" (longs for) — a subtle but real sense-shift. "willeth" is independently attested in KJV.db (Romans 9:16, "not of him that willeth") and is the majority witness choice (Webster, Darby, YLT all read "will(eth)").
- NEW: The wind bloweth where it willeth, and thou hearest the sound thereof, but canst not tell whence it cometh, and whither it goeth: so is every one that is born of the Spirit.

## listeth → desireth — James 3:4
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Same reasoning as John 3:8 — "willeth" is better attested and matches Webster/Darby's readings ("governor willeth").
- NEW: Behold also the ships, which though they be so great, and are driven of fierce winds, yet are they turned about with a very small helm, whithersoever the governor willeth.

## litters → covers — Isaiah 66:20
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "in covers" names no known vehicle and is nonsensical. Darby's "covered waggons" points to "wagons" (9 hits), a genuine, well-attested vehicle-word.
- NEW: And they shall bring all your brethren for an offering unto the Lord out of all nations upon horses, and in chariots, and in wagons, and upon mules, and upon swift beasts, to my holy mountain Jerusalem, saith the Lord, as the children of Israel bring an offering in a clean vessel into the house of the Lord.

## loaden → laden — Isaiah 46:1
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "laden" is merely a spelling variant of the flagged word "loaden" (same lemma), not a genuinely different word, per the grammar-rules memory. "burdened" (2 independent hits: II Corinthians 5:4, 8:13) is a real, distinct, attested alternative preserving the sense.
- NEW: Bel boweth down, Nebo stoopeth, their idols were upon the beasts, and upon the cattle: your carriages were heavily burdened; they are a burden to the weary beast.

## loadeth → ladeth — Psalms 68:19
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "ladeth" is merely a spelling variant of "loadeth," not a genuinely different word. ASV/BSB's "beareth our burden" is well-attested (27 hits for "beareth") and preserves the sense of God's daily provision.
- NEW: Blessed be the Lord, who daily beareth our burden, even the God of our salvation. Selah.

## loan → petition — I Samuel 2:20
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "petition" reflects a legitimate alternate rendering directly supported by three witnesses (Geneva, YLT, ASV all read "petition" here, understanding the verse as referring to Hannah's prayer-request rather than a "loan").
- NEW: And Eli blessed Elkanah and his wife, and said, The Lord give thee seed of this woman for the petition which is lent to the Lord. And they went unto their own home.

## loathe → lothe — Job 7:16
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "lothe" is merely a spelling variant of "loathe," not a genuinely different word. Geneva's "abhorre" points to "abhor" (45 hits), a well-attested and genuinely distinct synonym.
- NEW: I abhor it; I would not live alway: let me alone; for my days are vanity.

## loatheth → treadeth — Numbers 21:5
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "treadeth this light bread" is nonsensical here — trampling is not the sense of aversion to food that the context requires (contrast with Proverbs 27:7 below, where treading fits). "abhorreth" (5 hits) is a genuinely distinct, well-attested word preserving the sense of revulsion.
- NEW: And the people spake against God, and against Moses, Wherefore have ye brought us up out of Egypt to die in the wilderness? for there is no bread, neither is there any water; and our soul abhorreth this light bread.

## loatheth → treadeth — Proverbs 27:7
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Unlike Numbers 21:5, "treadeth" makes coherent sense here (a satiated person tramples what they no longer crave) and is directly attested by both DRC ("shall tread upon the honeycomb") and YLT ("treadeth down a honeycomb").
- NEW: The full soul treadeth an honeycomb; but to the hungry soul every bitter thing is sweet.

## lock → locks — Song of Solomon 5:5
- verdict: REVISE
