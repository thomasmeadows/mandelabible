# Rare word witness batch 0060 — proposals

## lurk → hid — Proverbs 1:18
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Same grammar fix as batch 0059's Proverbs 1:11 — "let us hid" is wrong inflection; "let us hide" is correct after "let us."
- NEW: And they lay wait for their own blood; they hide privily for their own lives.

## lusty → fat — Judges 3:29
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "fat" is directly attested by Darby's independent rendering of this exact verse ("all fat"), and period English used "fat" for well-fed, vigorous men, matching Geneva's "fed men."
- NEW: And they slew of Moab at that time about ten thousand men, all fat, and all men of valour; and there escaped not a man.

## magician → magicians — Daniel 2:10
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR merely pluralizes the flagged word (same lemma). "enchanter" (2 hits) is genuinely distinct and matches Geneva's "inchanter" for this role.
- NEW: The Chaldeans answered before the king, and said, There is not a man upon the earth that can shew the king's matter: therefore there is no king, lord, nor ruler, that asked such things at any enchanter, or astrologer, or Chaldean.

## magistrate → drive — Judges 18:7
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "there was no drive in the land" is nonsensical. "ruler" (161 hits) is extremely well-attested and matches the sense in ASV/Darby ("none...possessing authority").
- NEW: Then the five men departed, and came to Laish, and saw the people that were therein, how they dwelt careless, after the manner of the Zidonians, quiet and secure; and there was no ruler in the land, that might put them to shame in any thing; and they were far from the Zidonians, and had no business with any man.

## magistrate → drive — Luke 12:58

- rationale: "to the drive" is nonsensical. "ruler" matches Tyndale ("the ruler") and YLT directly.
- NEW: When thou goest with thine adversary to the ruler, as thou art in the way, give diligence that thou mayest be delivered from him; lest he hale thee to the judge, and the judge deliver thee to the officer, and the officer cast thee into prison.

## magnificence → mighty — Acts 19:27
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "her mighty should be destroyed" is ungrammatical (bare adjective used as noun). "greatness" (35 hits) matches Darby and YLT directly.
- NEW: So that not only this our craft is in danger to be set at nought; but also that the temple of the great goddess Diana should be despised, and her greatness should be destroyed, whom all Asia and the world worshippeth.

## mail → coat — I Samuel 17:5

- rationale: CUR's "a coat of coat" duplicates the word nonsensically. "scales" (9 hits) matches Darby's "corselet of scales" and DRC's "coat of mail with scales" directly.
- NEW: And he had an helmet of brass upon his head, and he was armed with a coat of scales; and the weight of the coat was five thousand shekels of brass.

## mail → coat — I Samuel 17:38

## mainsail → sail — Acts 27:40
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "sail" is extremely common, genuinely distinct from "mainsail," and preserves the essential sense even if less nautically precise than ASV/Darby's "foresail" (which is itself unattested in KJV.db).
- NEW: And when they had taken up the anchors, they committed themselves unto the sea, and loosed the rudder bands, and hoised up the sail to the wind, and made toward shore.

- NEW: They answered and said unto him, If he were not an evildoer, we would not have delivered him up unto thee.

## mansions → habitations — John 14:2

## manslayer → murderer — Numbers 35:6
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "murderer" directly contradicts the legal point of the passage — cities of refuge exist precisely to protect the unintentional killer (manslayer) FROM the avenger, as distinct from a murderer who acted with intent. "slayer" (22 hits) preserves the neutral sense without importing "intentional killing."
- NEW: And among the cities which ye shall give unto the Levites there shall be six cities for refuge, which ye shall appoint for the slayer, that he may flee thither: and to them ye shall add forty and two cities.

## manslayer → murderer — Numbers 35:12
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Same legal-meaning problem as Numbers 35:6 — REVISE to "slayer" for consistency within the same chapter's legal terminology.
- NEW: And they shall be unto you cities for refuge from the avenger; that the slayer die not, until he stand before the congregation in judgment.

## marchedst → march — Judges 5:4
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR drops the -st ending, breaking thou-agreement and merely re-inflecting the same lemma. The periphrastic "thou didst march" (RWebster's own choice) restores grammar.
- NEW: Lord, when thou wentest out of Seir, when thou didst march out of the field of Edom, the earth trembled, and the heavens dropped, the clouds also dropped water.
