# Rare word witness batch 0067 — proposals

## offscouring → refuse — I Corinthians 4:13
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "waste" loses the force of the image. DRC renders "the refuse of this world"; "refuse" is well attested in the KJV corpus (67 distinct verses, e.g. Amos 8:6 "the refuse of the wheat") and preserves the sense of cast-off filth.
- NEW: Being defamed, we intreat: we are made as the filth of the world, and are the refuse of all things unto this day.

## oftener → oftentimes — Acts 24:26
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "the often" is ungrammatical (drops the comparative sense). DRC reads "oftentimes sending for him"; "oftentimes" is genuinely attested in the KJV corpus (6 distinct verses, e.g. Mark, Ecclesiastes) and carries the same repeated-summons meaning without needing "the."
- NEW: He hoped also that money should have been given him of Paul, that he might loose him: wherefore he sent for him oftentimes, and communed with him.

## oldness → old letter — Romans 7:6
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR is identical to OLD — the rare word was never actually replaced. Tyndale rephrases the abstract noun away entirely ("the olde conversacion of the letter"); following that model, dropping the "-ness" abstraction in favor of the plain adjective avoids the flagged word while preserving Paul's antithesis with "newness of spirit."
- NEW: But now we are delivered from the law, that being dead wherein we were held; that we should serve in newness of spirit, and not in the old letter.

## oliveyard → olive trees — Exodus 23:11
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "thy olive" is missing its noun. Geneva1599 reads "thine oliue trees"; "olive trees" is well attested in the KJV corpus (7 distinct verses, e.g. Judges, Psalm 128:3 "olive plants").
- NEW: But the seventh year thou shalt let it rest and lie still; that the poor of thy people may eat: and what they leave the beasts of the field shall eat. In like manner thou shalt deal with thy vineyard, and with thy olive trees.

## omitted → left undone — Matthew 23:23
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "have suffered" inverts the meaning (suffered = allowed, the opposite of omitted/neglected). Wycliffe/DRC/Tyndale all render "left [undone]," and the KJV's own text in this very verse already uses "leave the other undone" — "left undone" matches that internal construction exactly.
- NEW: Woe unto you, scribes and Pharisees, hypocrites! for ye pay tithe of mint and anise and cummin, and have left undone the weightier matters of the law, judgment, mercy, and faith: these ought ye to have done, and not to leave the other undone.

## omnipotent → almighty — Revelation of John 19:6
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "almighty" is a genuinely different word from "omnipotent," construction is unchanged ("the Lord God almighty reigneth"), and DRC/ASV/YLT all render the underlying word as "the Almighty" — sound choice already in place.
- NEW: And I heard as it were the voice of a great multitude, and as the voice of many waters, and as the voice of mighty thunderings, saying, Alleluia: for the Lord God almighty reigneth.

## onions → onions (flag is spurious) — Numbers 11:5
- verdict: NO-SAFE-SWAP
- rationale: "onions" is already the KJV's own word here, and it is independently attested identically in Wycliffe ("oyniouns"), Geneva1599, and DRC — a real ANE foodstuff with no other period name. The flag is spurious; CUR was already left unchanged, so no action is needed beyond confirming no swap is safe or necessary.
- NEW: We remember the fish, which we did eat in Egypt freely; the cucumbers, and the melons, and the leeks, and the onions, and the garlick:

## onward → forward — Exodus 40:36
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "forward" is a genuinely different, common word, matches Geneva1599's own rendering exactly ("went forward"), and requires no grammar changes.
- NEW: And when the cloud was taken up from over the tabernacle, the children of Israel went forward in all their journeys:

## onycha → onycha (flag is spurious) — Exodus 30:34
- verdict: NO-SAFE-SWAP
- rationale: "onycha" is a technical spice-ingredient name attested unanimously across witnesses (Wycliffe "onyca," DRC/Geneva/others "onycha" or close cognates) with no true synonym; the King James agent's own note already found no safe swap, and CUR correctly left it unchanged.
- NEW: And the Lord said unto Moses, Take unto thee sweet spices, stacte, and onycha, and galbanum; these sweet spices with pure frankincense: of each shall there be a like weight:

## oppose → resist — II Timothy 2:25
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR duplicates "themselves themselves," a broken non-word repetition. DRC reads "them that resist the truth"; "resist" is well attested in the KJV corpus (e.g. James 4:7, Romans 13:2) and needs no reflexive pronoun.
- NEW: In meekness instructing those that resist; if God peradventure will give them repentance to the acknowledging of the truth;

## opposed → resisted — Acts 18:6
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "resisted themselves" is ungrammatical — one cannot "resist" oneself in this sense. Geneva1599 reads "when they resisted, and blasphemed" with no reflexive pronoun; that construction is adopted directly.
- NEW: And when they resisted, and blasphemed, he shook his raiment, and said unto them, Your blood be upon your own heads; I am clean: from henceforth I will go unto the Gentiles.

## opposest → persecutest — Job 30:21
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "thou hatest thyself against me" is both ungrammatical and semantically wrong (a reflexive "hatest thyself" makes no sense in this adversarial context). ASV independently reads "thou persecutest me" — genuinely different from "opposest," and "persecutest" is one of the most recognizable second-person forms in the KJV idiom (echoing Acts 9:4, "Saul, Saul, why persecutest thou me?").
- NEW: Thou art become cruel to me: with thy strong hand thou persecutest me.

## opposeth → resisteth — II Thessalonians 2:4
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "resisteth" is a genuinely different, well-attested KJV word (e.g. Romans 13:2 "resisteth the power") and the sentence is grammatically sound as given.
- NEW: Who resisteth and exalteth himself above all that is called God, or that is worshipped; so that he as God sitteth in the temple of God, shewing himself that he is God.

## oppositions → gainsayings — I Timothy 6:20
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "gainsayings" is genuinely different from "oppositions" and is attested in the KJV corpus (e.g. Jude 1:11 "gainsaying of Core," Romans 10:21 "gainsaying people").
- NEW: O Timothy, keep that which is committed to thy trust, avoiding profane and vain babblings, and gainsayings of science falsely so called:

## oration → speech — Acts 12:21
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR keeps the right word but the wrong article ("an speech" before a consonant sound). "speech" is a common, well-attested KJV noun (e.g. Exodus 4:10); only the article needs correcting.
- NEW: And upon a set day Herod, arrayed in royal apparel, sat upon his throne, and made a speech unto them.

## orator → enchanter — Isaiah 3:3
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "eloquent enchantment" pairs an adjective with the wrong noun class and is nonsensical. Darby reads "one versed in enchantments," ASV/ACV "the skilful enchanter" — "enchanter" is attested in the KJV corpus (e.g. Daniel 2:2) and fits "eloquent" naturally.
- NEW: The captain of fifty, and the honourable man, and the counsellor, and the cunning artificer, and the eloquent enchanter.

## orator → advocate — Acts 24:1
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "a certain prayer named Tertullus" mistakes the noun for the wrong word class entirely — a person cannot be named "prayer." No witness offers an alternate word for this legal speaker, but "advocate" is attested in the KJV corpus (1 John 2:1, "we have an advocate with the Father") and fits Tertullus's role as Paul's legal accuser before the governor.
- NEW: And after five days Ananias the high priest descended with the elders, and with a certain advocate named Tertullus, who informed the governor against Paul.

## orchard → paradise — Song of Solomon 4:13
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "an for" is a broken non-word. Wycliffe/DRC both read "paradise of pomegranates," and "paradise" is attested in the KJV corpus (e.g. Luke 23:43, 2 Corinthians 12:4, Revelation 2:7); article corrected to "a" before the consonant sound.
- NEW: Thy plants are a paradise of pomegranates, with pleasant fruits; camphire, with spikenard,

## orchards → (rephrased) — Ecclesiastes 2:5
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "forests" names the wrong referent (a forest is not a cultivated orchard). No witness offers a period-authentic single-word substitute distinct from "orchard/park," so the clause is rephrased to fold the redundant noun into the following relative clause, per the "not one-for-one" grammar ruling — the planted-fruit-trees clause already carries the full sense.
- NEW: I made me gardens, and I planted trees in them of all kind of fruits:

## orderings → courses — I Chronicles 24:19
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "officers" wrongly names people rather than the arrangement of their service. DRC reads "their courses according to their ministries," and "courses" is the KJV's own term used repeatedly throughout this very chapter (1 Chronicles 24) for the priestly divisions — the strongest possible internal match.
- NEW: These were the courses of them in their service to come into the house of the Lord, according to their manner, under Aaron their father, as the Lord God of Israel had commanded him.

## orderly → circumspectly — Acts 21:24
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "circumspectly" is a genuinely different word and is directly attested in the KJV corpus (Ephesians 5:15, "walk circumspectly") — an excellent internal match.
- NEW: Them take, and purify thyself with them, and be at charges with them, that they may shave their heads: and all may know that those things, whereof they were informed concerning thee, are nothing; but that thou thyself also walkest circumspectly, and keepest the law.

## ordinary → appointed food — Ezekiel 16:27
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "thine statute food" reads as an awkward noun-pile. Darby reads "diminished thine appointed portion"; "appointed" is very common in the KJV corpus (131 distinct verses) and combines naturally with "food," which the verse already supplies.
- NEW: Behold, therefore I have stretched out my hand over thee, and have diminished thine appointed food, and delivered thee unto the will of them that hate thee, the daughters of the Philistines, which are ashamed of thy lewd way.

## orphans → fatherless, even without father — Lamentations 5:3

- rationale: CUR "fatherless and fatherless" is a broken literal duplicate, not a real replacement. Geneva1599 reads "We are fatherles, euen without father," which conveys the doubled emphasis of the Hebrew idiom without repeating the flagged word.
- NEW: We are fatherless, even without father: our mothers are as widows.
