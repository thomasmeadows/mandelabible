# Rare word witness batch 0068 — proposals

## ostrich → ostrich (flag is spurious) — Job 39:13
- verdict: NO-SAFE-SWAP
- rationale: CUR "wings and feathers unto the feathers" is a broken duplicate. "ostrich" is a real, unique ANE bird with no synonym, and Wycliffe attests the identical word ("ostriche"); the flag is spurious. Restoring OLD.
- NEW: Gavest thou the goodly wings unto the peacocks? or wings and feathers unto the ostrich?

## ostriches → ostriches (flag is spurious) — Lamentations 4:3
- verdict: NO-SAFE-SWAP
- rationale: CUR "like the like" is a broken duplicate. "ostriches" is attested identically in Wycliffe ("ostrig"), Geneva1599, and DRC — a real bird with no synonym; the King James agent's own note already found no safe swap. Restoring OLD.
- NEW: Even the sea monsters draw out the breast, they give suck to their young ones: the daughter of my people is become cruel, like the ostriches in the wilderness.

## outlandish → strange — Nehemiah 13:26
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "strange" is genuinely different from "outlandish," matches Geneva1599 exactly ("strange women"), and is common, well-attested KJV idiom (cf. Proverbs' "strange woman").
- NEW: Did not Solomon king of Israel sin by these things? yet among many nations was there no king like him, who was beloved of his God, and God made him king over all Israel: nevertheless even him did strange women cause to sin.

## outlived → whose days were prolonged — Judges 2:7
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "elders that required Joshua" is nonsensical. Darby/YLT independently render "whose days were prolonged after Joshua" — a genuinely different, well-attested construction ("prolong...days" is common KJV idiom, e.g. Deuteronomy 4:26, Isaiah 53:10).
- NEW: And the people served the Lord all the days of Joshua, and all the days of the elders whose days were prolonged after Joshua, who had seen all the great works of the Lord, that he did for Israel.

## outrageous → raging — Proverbs 27:4
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "anger is waters" is nonsensical. Geneva1599 reads "wrath is raging"; "raging" is directly attested elsewhere in the same book (Proverbs 20:1, "strong drink is raging"), the strongest possible internal match.
- NEW: Wrath is cruel, and anger is raging; but who is able to stand before envy?

## outrun → run before — John 20:4
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "did ran Peter" is ungrammatical and drops the sense of racing ahead. Wycliffe reads "ran bifor Petre" ("ran before Peter"); "before" is common throughout the KJV corpus, giving a genuinely different and grammatical construction.
- NEW: So they ran both together: and the other disciple did run before Peter, and came first to the sepulchre.

## outwardly → on the outside — Matthew 23:28
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "openly" nearly inverts the sense — Jesus contrasts outward show with inward hypocrisy, not secrecy with disclosure. This same discourse already uses "outside" for the identical contrast three verses earlier (Matthew 23:25, "make clean the outside of the cup"), giving the strongest possible internal precedent for a genuinely different phrase.
- NEW: Even so ye also appear righteous unto men on the outside, but within ye are full of hypocrisy and iniquity.

## outwardly → outward — Romans 2:28
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "openly" is a weaker, slightly different sense (visible vs. public) and duplicates the risk of the Matthew 23:28 entry. The same verse already uses "outward" (unflagged) in its second clause ("outward in the flesh"); repeating that word for the first clause gives a genuinely different part of speech than the flagged adverb and matches the verse's own internal parallelism.
- NEW: For he is not a Jew, which is one outward; neither is that circumcision, which is outward in the flesh:

## outwent → went before — Mark 6:33
- verdict: REVISE

- NEW: And the people saw them departing, and many knew him, and ran afoot thither out of all cities, and went before them, and came together unto him.

## overpass → exceed — Jeremiah 5:28
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "pass" drops the "over-" sense entirely and reads as ambiguous ("pass the deeds," not "surpass" them), whereas UKJV's own rendering here is "pass over." RWebster's "exceed" is genuinely different, unambiguous, and attested elsewhere in the KJV (e.g. 2 Corinthians 3:9, "exceed in glory").
- NEW: They are waxen fat, they shine: yea, they exceed the deeds of the wicked: they judge not the cause, the cause of the fatherless, yet they prosper; and the right of the needy do they not judge.

## overplus → remainder — Leviticus 25:27
- verdict: REVISE

- NEW: Then let him count the years of the sale thereof, and restore the remainder unto the man to whom he sold it; that he may return unto his possession.

- NEW: But with an overwhelming flood he will make an utter end of the place thereof, and darkness shall pursue his enemies.

## overshadow → overshadow (flag is spurious) — Luke 1:35
- verdict: NO-SAFE-SWAP
- rationale: CUR "shall might thee" is nonsensical. "overshadow" is unanimously attested across every witness with zero variation (Wycliffe "ouerschadewe," Tyndale "over shaddowe," Geneva1599 "ouershadowe," DRC "overshadow") and carries a deliberate echo of the Exodus cloud of God's presence; no synonym would preserve that resonance. The flag is spurious. Restoring OLD.
- NEW: And the angel answered and said unto her, The Holy Ghost shall come upon thee, and the power of the Highest shall overshadow thee: therefore also that holy thing which shall be born of thee shall be called the Son of God.
