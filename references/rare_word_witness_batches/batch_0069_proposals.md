# Rare word witness batch 0069 — proposals

## paces → steps — II Samuel 6:13
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "steps" is genuinely different from "paces" and matches YLT and BBE's independent renderings ("stepped six steps"/"gone six steps").
- NEW: And it was so, that when they that bare the ark of the Lord had gone six steps, he sacrificed oxen and fatlings.

## pacified → appeased — Esther 7:10
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "purged" is the wrong concept entirely (purged ≠ calmed). Darby independently reads "the king's wrath was appeased"; "appeased" is genuinely different and attested in the KJV (Genesis 32:20, Acts 19:35).
- NEW: So they hanged Haman on the gallows that he had prepared for Mordecai. Then was the king's wrath appeased.

## pacified → appeased — Ezekiel 16:63
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Same reasoning as Esther 7:10 — "purged toward thee" is ungrammatical and wrong in sense; "appeased" is genuinely different and well attested.
- NEW: That thou mayest remember, and be confounded, and never open thy mouth any more because of thy shame, when I am appeased toward thee for all that thou hast done, saith the Lord God.

## pacifieth → quencheth — Proverbs 21:14
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "remaineth" nearly inverts the sense. Wycliffe/DRC read "quencheth"; "quench" is genuinely different and well attested in the KJV (Matthew 12:20, Ephesians 6:16, Song of Solomon 8:7).
- NEW: A gift in secret quencheth anger: and a reward in the bosom strong wrath.

## pacifieth → appeaseth — Ecclesiastes 10:4
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "remaineth great offences" is nonsensical. "appeaseth" is genuinely different, matches the calming sense of YLT's "quieteth"/ASV's "allayeth," and keeps consistency with the other "pacify"-family entries in this batch.
- NEW: If the spirit of the ruler rise up against thee, leave not thy place; for yielding appeaseth great offences.

## pacify → appease — Proverbs 16:14
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "will atonement it" is ungrammatical (a noun used as a verb). "appease" is genuinely different, matches the sense of Darby's own rendering, and stays consistent with the batch's other pacify-family fixes.
- NEW: The wrath of a king is as messengers of death: but a wise man will appease it.

## paddle → nail — Deuteronomy 23:13

- rationale: "nail" is genuinely different from "paddle" and is independently attested by YLT ("a nail thou hast on thy staff").
- NEW: And thou shalt have a nail upon thy weapon; and it shall be, when thou wilt ease thyself abroad, thou shalt dig therewith, and shalt turn back and cover that which cometh from thee:

## painful → grievous — Psalms 73:16
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "too labour for me" is ungrammatical (a noun where an adjective is required). Darby's "a grievous task" points to "grievous," genuinely different and extremely common throughout the KJV.
- NEW: When I thought to know this, it was too grievous for me;

## painfulness → travail — II Corinthians 11:27
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "travail" is genuinely different and independently attested by ASV ("in labor and travail").
- NEW: In weariness and travail, in watchings often, in hunger and thirst, in fastings often, in cold and nakedness.

## painted → stained — II Kings 9:30
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "anointed her face" wrongly imports religious/ceremonial anointing where the text describes cosmetic makeup — a real change of meaning. "stained" is genuinely different, well attested in the KJV (Isaiah 63:3, "I will stain all my raiment"; Job 3:5), and fits the application of red pigment to skin.
- NEW: And when Jehu was come to Jezreel, Jezebel heard of it; and she stained her face, and tired her head, and looked out at a window.

## painted → stained — Jeremiah 22:14
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Same reasoning as II Kings 9:30 — CUR "anointed with vermilion" misapplies ceremonial anointing to a house wall. "stained" is genuinely different and fits a red pigment being applied to woodwork.
- NEW: That saith, I will build me a wide house and large chambers, and cutteth him out windows; and it is cieled with cedar, and stained with vermilion.

## paintedst → stainedst — Ezekiel 23:40
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "thyself thy eyes" is a broken non-word duplicate. Using "stainedst," the second-person form of the "stained" swap adopted at II Kings 9:30 and Jeremiah 22:14, keeps this entry consistent with its sibling verses and is genuinely different from "paintedst."
- NEW: And furthermore, that ye have sent for men to come from far, unto whom a messenger was sent; and, lo, they came: for whom thou didst wash thyself, stainedst thine eyes, and deckedst thyself with ornaments,

## painting → colour — Jeremiah 4:30
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "with colouring" is an awkward, weakly attested form. "colour" as a plain noun is well attested in the KJV (repeatedly in Leviticus 13, e.g. "if the colour be white"), and substitutes cleanly into the same clause structure.
- NEW: And when thou art spoiled, what wilt thou do? Though thou clothest thyself with crimson, though thou deckest thee with ornaments of gold, though thou rentest thy face with colour, in vain shalt thou make thyself fair; thy lovers will despise thee, they will seek thy life.

## pale → pale (flag is spurious) — Isaiah 29:22
- verdict: NO-SAFE-SWAP
- rationale: CUR "wax green" wrongly changes the color entirely. "pale"/"paleness" recurs independently elsewhere in this very batch (Revelation 6:8, Jeremiah 30:6) with no witness offering a consistent alternative, showing it is ordinary period vocabulary rather than a genuine alteration artifact. No synonym for this fear-idiom is offered by any witness. Restoring OLD.
- NEW: Therefore thus saith the Lord, who redeemed Abraham, concerning the house of Jacob, Jacob shall not now be ashamed, neither shall his face now wax pale.

## pale → green — Revelation of John 6:8
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Unlike the Isaiah 29:22 entry, here CUR's "green" has direct period-witness support: Tyndale independently reads "a grene horsse." The underlying Greek chloros (source of "chlorophyll") denotes a pale yellow-green, so "green" is arguably the more source-faithful rendering as well as a genuinely different, attested word.
- NEW: And I looked, and behold a green horse: and his name that sat on him was Death, and Hell followed with him. And power was given unto them over the fourth part of the earth, to kill with sword, and with hunger, and with death, and with the beasts of the earth.

## paleness → whiteness — Jeremiah 30:6
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "whiteness" is genuinely different from "paleness" and expresses a plausible period idiom for a fear-blanched face; the underlying concept (face changing color with fear) is confirmed by BBE ("turned green") and Wycliffe/DRC ("turned yellow"), all pointing to color change rather than requiring the exact word "pale."
- NEW: Ask ye now, and see whether a man doth travail with child? wherefore do I see every man with his hands on his loins, as a woman in travail, and all faces are turned into whiteness?

## panted → panted (flag is spurious) — Psalms 119:131

## paper → paper (flag is spurious) — II John 1:12

- NEW: Having many things to write unto you, I would not write with paper and ink: but I trust to come unto you, and speak face to face, that our joy may be full.
