# Rare word witness batch 0071 — proposals

## perfectness → perfection — Colossians 3:14
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "perfection" is genuinely different from "perfectness" and matches DRC, Wycliffe, ACV, and UKJV's independent renderings.
- NEW: And above all these things put on charity, which is the bond of perfection.

## performance → fulfilling — Luke 1:45
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "a perform" is ungrammatical (a bare verb used as a noun). Darby/ASV/ACV independently render "a fulfilment"; using "fulfilling" (matching the KJV's own noun form at Romans 13:10, "fulfilling of the law") gives a genuinely different, grammatical, and internally consistent word.
- NEW: And blessed is she that believed: for there shall be a fulfilling of those things which were told her from the Lord.

## performance → fulfilling — II Corinthians 8:11
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Same reasoning as Luke 1:45 — CUR "a perform" is ungrammatical; "fulfilling" is genuinely different and grammatical.
- NEW: Now therefore perform the doing of it; that as there was a readiness to will, so there may be a fulfilling also out of that which ye have.

## peril → risk — Lamentations 5:9
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "the perils" merely pluralizes the flagged word — a re-inflection, not a genuine replacement. Darby independently reads "at the risk of our lives," matching BSB; "risk" is genuinely different.
- NEW: We gat our bread with the risk of our lives because of the sword of the wilderness.

## peril → danger — Romans 8:35
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "or perils" merely pluralizes the flagged word. Darby/BBE independently read "danger"; "danger" is genuinely different and fits the list construction.
- NEW: Who shall separate us from the love of Christ? shall tribulation, or distress, or persecution, or famine, or nakedness, or danger, or sword?

## perilous → dangerous — II Timothy 3:1
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "evil times" loses the specific sense of danger. DRC and UKJV independently read "dangerous times"; "dangerous" is genuinely different and a closer match to the original sense.
- NEW: This know also, that in the last days dangerous times shall come.

- NEW: For I will not see you now by the way; but I trust to tarry a while with you, if the Lord suffer.

## pernicious → destructive — II Peter 2:2

## persecutor → an oppressor — I Timothy 1:13
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "a oppressor" has the wrong article before a vowel sound. Geneva1599 independently uses "oppresser" as a distinct third term alongside "persecuter," confirming it as a genuine, different period word; only the article needed correcting.
- NEW: Who was before a blasphemer, and an oppressor, and injurious: but I obtained mercy, because I did it ignorantly in unbelief.

## perseverance → patience — Ephesians 6:18

- NEW: Praying always with all prayer and supplication in the Spirit, and watching thereunto with all patience and supplication for all saints;

## persuasion → persuasion (flag is spurious) — Galatians 5:8
- verdict: NO-SAFE-SWAP
- rationale: "persuasion" is attested unanimously across witnesses (Geneva1599 "perswasion," DRC, Webster, RWebster, ASV, UKJV, ACV, BSB), confirming it as genuine period vocabulary with no synonym. The King James agent's own note already found no safe swap.
- NEW: This persuasion cometh not of him that calleth you.

## pestilent → a pestilence — Acts 24:5
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "a pestilence fellow" is ungrammatical (a noun misused as an adjective). YLT independently uses the grammatical appositive construction "found this man a pestilence"; dropping "fellow" and adopting YLT's exact construction gives a genuinely different part of speech (noun rather than adjective) and fixes the grammar.
- NEW: For we have found this man a pestilence, and a mover of sedition among all the Jews throughout the world, and a ringleader of the sect of the Nazarenes:
