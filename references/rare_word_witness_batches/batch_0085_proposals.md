# Rare word witness batch 0085 — proposals

## satiate → fill — Jeremiah 31:14
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR "I will full the soul" uses "full" as a verb, which is not standard English and breaks grammar. DRC/Wycliffe render this exact verse "I will fill the soul of the priests"; "fill" is common in the corpus (50 hits) and preserves the meaning without inverting it.
- NEW: And I will fill the soul of the priests with fatness, and my people shall be satisfied with my goodness, saith the Lord.

## satiate → full — Jeremiah 46:10
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Here CUR's "full" works grammatically as a predicate adjective ("it shall be full...with their blood"), unlike the verb-slot problem in Jeremiah 31:14. It is genuinely different from "satiate", common, and preserves the meaning of the sword being sated with blood without inverting it.
- NEW: For this is the day of the Lord God of hosts, a day of vengeance, that he may avenge him of his adversaries: and the sword shall devour, and it shall be full and made drunk with their blood: for the Lord God of hosts hath a sacrifice in the north country by the river Euphrates.
