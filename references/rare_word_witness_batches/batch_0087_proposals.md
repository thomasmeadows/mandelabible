## seemly → comely — Proverbs 26:1
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "comely" is well-attested in KJV (18 verses) and matches YLT's own choice exactly.
- NEW: As snow in summer, and as rain in harvest, so honour is not comely for a fool.

## selfwill → favour — Genesis 49:6
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "favour" inverts the sense (a positive word for a violent act). Darby reads "in their wantonness they houghed oxen"; "wantonness" is attested in KJV (Romans 13:13, 2 Peter 2:18) and preserves the negative sense of unchecked will.
- NEW: O my soul, come not thou into their secret; unto their assembly, mine honour, be not thou united: for in their anger they slew a man, and in their wantonness they digged down a wall.

## selfwilled → stubborn — Titus 1:7
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "stubborn" is a genuinely different, well-attested KJV word (Deuteronomy 21:18, 1 Samuel 15:23) fitting the sense of headstrong obstinacy.
- NEW: For a bishop must be blameless, as the steward of God; not stubborn, not soon angry, not given to wine, no striker, not given to filthy lucre;

## selfwilled → stubborn — II Peter 2:10
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: same word, consistent with Titus 1:7 ruling above.
- NEW: But chiefly them that walk after the flesh in the lust of uncleanness, and despise government. Presumptuous are they, stubborn, they are not afraid to speak evil of dignities.

## sense → wisdom — Nehemiah 8:8
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "wisdom" mistakes the sense — the Levites were explaining the text's meaning, not imparting wisdom generally. YLT reads "give the meaning"; "meaning" is attested in KJV (e.g. Daniel 8:15) and preserves the sense precisely.
- NEW: So they read in the book in the law of God distinctly, and gave the meaning, and caused them to understand the reading.

## seraphims → serpents — Isaiah 6:2
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "serpents" inverts the meaning outright — these are winged heavenly attendants with faces and feet, not snakes; the swap contradicts the verse's own description. "angels" is common throughout KJV (Genesis 19:1, etc.) and preserves the sense of heavenly beings without inventing new content.
- NEW: Above it stood the angels: each one had six wings; with twain he covered his face, and with twain he covered his feet, and with twain he did fly.

## servedst → serve — Deuteronomy 28:47
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "serve" is a disguised re-inflection of the flagged word (servedst is simply "serve" stripped of its ending), and it breaks the second-person past tense entirely ("thou serve not" is ungrammatical). DRC reads "thou didst not serve"; using a genuinely different verb, "worship" (common, 199 verses), with the auxiliary carrying the tense avoids both problems.
- NEW: Because thou didst not worship the Lord thy God with joyfulness, and with gladness of heart, for the abundance of all things;

## setter → forth — Acts 17:18
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR is broken ("a forth forth of strange gods" is nonsense, a duplicated word). BBE reads "a preacher of strange gods"; "preacher" is attested in KJV (Ecclesiastes's title, 2 Peter 2:5, 1 Timothy 2:7) and restores sound grammar and meaning.
- NEW: Then certain philosophers of the Epicureans, and of the Stoicks, encountered him. And some said, What will this babbler say? other some, He seemeth to be a preacher of strange gods: because he preached unto them Jesus, and the resurrection.

## severally → privately — I Corinthians 12:11
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "privately" mistakes "severally" (individually) for "secretly," the wrong sense entirely. "particularly" is attested in KJV (Acts 21:19, "declared particularly") and matches the individuating sense correctly.
- NEW: But all these worketh that one and the selfsame Spirit, dividing to every man particularly as he will.

## severity → sharpness — Romans 11:22
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR cures one rare word (severity, 1 occurrence) with another equally rare word (sharpness, 1 occurrence) — a circular swap forbidden by the "don't cure rare with rare" rule. "rigour" is better attested (5 verses, e.g. Leviticus 25:43 "rule over him with rigour") and fits the sense of stern, exacting judgment.
- NEW: Behold therefore the goodness and rigour of God: on them which fell, rigour; but toward thee, goodness, if thou continue in his goodness: otherwise thou also shalt be cut off.

## sew → stitch — Ecclesiastes 3:7
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "stitch" has zero occurrences in the KJV corpus (forbidden by rule 4). "join" is attested (14 verses) and captures the sense opposite to "rend" (tear apart).
- NEW: A time to rend, and a time to join; a time to keep silence, and a time to speak;

## sew → stitch — Ezekiel 13:18
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: same problem as Ecclesiastes 3:7; Wycliffe itself reads "sowen togidere" (joined together), supporting "join" as the period-authentic construction.
- NEW: And say, Thus saith the Lord God; Woe to the women that join pillows to all armholes, and make kerchiefs upon the head of every stature to hunt souls! Will ye hunt the souls of my people, and will ye save the souls alive that come unto you?
