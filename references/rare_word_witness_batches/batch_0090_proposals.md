## signification → dumb — I Corinthians 14:10
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's construction is broken ("without dumb" is ungrammatical). Geneva reads "none of them is dumme" directly (without "without"); this construction is well-attested and preserves the meaning through equivalent double-negation.
- NEW: There are, it may be, so many kinds of voices in the world, and none of them is dumb.

- verdict: REVISE

- NEW: And this word, Yet once more, sheweth the removing of those things that are shaken, as of things that are made, that those things which cannot be shaken may remain.

## silversmith → coppersmith — Acts 19:24
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "coppersmith" contradicts the verse's own statement that Demetrius "made silver shrines" — a plain internal inconsistency. "smith" is attested in KJV (16 verses, e.g. Isaiah 44:12, 54:16), is a genuinely different word from "silversmith," and does not duplicate "craftsmen" already present later in the verse. (Combine with the "shrines → temples" fix in batch 0089 for the full verse.)
- NEW: For a certain man named Demetrius, a smith, which made silver temples for Diana, brought no small gain unto the craftsmen;

## sixscore → twelve — I Kings 9:14
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "twelve" is a tenfold numeric error (sixscore = 120, not 12) — a clear meaning inversion of the amount of gold sent. "an hundred and twenty" matches Webster and RWebster exactly and preserves the correct figure.
- NEW: And Hiram sent to the king an hundred and twenty talents of gold.

## sixscore → twelve — Jonah 4:11
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: same tenfold numeric error as I Kings 9:14 (120,000 persons corrupted to 12,000); "an hundred and twenty thousand" matches DRC, Webster, and Darby.
- NEW: And should not I spare Nineveh, that great city, wherein are more than an hundred and twenty thousand persons that cannot discern between their right hand and their left hand; and also much cattle?

## sixtyfold → threescore — Matthew 13:8
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "threescore" correctly preserves the number sixty (it is simply the period-English cardinal, common in KJV, 96 verses) even though it drops the "-fold" suffix present on the neighboring terms; this does not invert or corrupt the meaning.
- NEW: But other fell into good ground, and brought forth fruit, some an hundredfold, some threescore, some thirtyfold.

## skippedst → bemoan — Jeremiah 48:27
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR is nonsensical ("thou bemoan for joy" does not parse — bemoaning is grief, not joy). Webster and RWebster read "thou hast leaped for joy"/"thou didst leap for joy"; using the auxiliary "didst" with the attested bare verb "leap" avoids re-inflecting the flagged word while preserving the sense.
- NEW: For was not Israel a derision unto thee? was he found among thieves? for since thou spakest of him, thou didst leap for joy.

- NEW: The voice of my beloved! behold, he cometh leaping upon the mountains, dancing upon the hills.

## slackness → slothfulness — II Peter 3:9
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "slothfulness" is itself KJV-rare (2 occurrences, both independently flagged as rare words elsewhere in this project) — curing one rare word with another rare word is forbidden. "idleness" is attested (3 verses) and is a genuinely different, sufficiently common word.
- NEW: The Lord is not slack concerning his promise, as some men count idleness; but is longsuffering to us-ward, not willing that any should perish, but that all should come to repentance.
