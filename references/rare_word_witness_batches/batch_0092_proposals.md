# Rare word witness batch 0092 — proposals

## smoother → sweeter — Psalms 55:21
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Verse carries two flagged words (smoother, softer) needing distinct fixes. YLT renders the first clause "Sweeter than honey hath been his mouth," giving a period-plausible, KJV-attested (3x) comparative distinct from "smoother." "Softer" is fixed separately below to avoid duplication.
- NEW: The words of his mouth were sweeter than butter, but war was in his heart: his words were more gentle than oil, yet were they drawn swords.

## smoother → soft — Proverbs 5:3
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Geneva1599 reads "her mouth is more soft then oyle" — a genuinely different, KJV-attested word (soft, 7x) in a period-witnessed "more X than" construction.
- NEW: For the lips of a strange woman drop as an honeycomb, and her mouth is more soft than oil:

## snail → worm — Leviticus 11:30
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Witnesses disagree wildly on this creature's identity (rat, shrew, stellio, gecko), none giving a KJV-attested alternative word; "worm" (15x in KJV) is a defensible generic term for an unclean creeping thing and no safer swap exists.
- NEW: And the ferret, and the chameleon, and the lizard, and the worm, and the mole.

## snail → worm — Psalms 58:8
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Same creature-identity problem as Leviticus 11:30; no witness supplies a KJV-attested alternate noun for the melting/dissolving image, so the existing "worm" (15x) stands as the least-risk choice.
- NEW: As a worm which melteth, let every one of them pass away: like the untimely birth of a woman, that they may not see the sun.
