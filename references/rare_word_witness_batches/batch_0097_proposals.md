# Batch 0097 proposals

## stretchedst → heldest forth — Exodus 15:12
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR broke thou/-est agreement ("Thou stretch"). Wycliffe reads "heldist forth thin hond"; "heldest" is attested in the KJV corpus and preserves the thou/-est inflection and the sense of extending the hand.
- NEW: Thou heldest forth thy right hand, the earth swallowed them.

## striker → smiter — I Timothy 3:3
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: CUR's "smiter" is Wycliffe's exact rendering ("a smytere") and is a genuinely different, attested word preserving the sense (not violent).
- NEW: Not given to wine, no smiter, not greedy of filthy lucre; but patient, not a brawler, not covetous;

## striker → smiter — Titus 1:7
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: Same as I Timothy 3:3 — Wycliffe "smytere" supports CUR.
- NEW: For a bishop must be blameless, as the steward of God; not selfwilled, not soon angry, not given to wine, no smiter, not given to filthy lucre;

## string → cord — Psalms 11:2
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "exceeding" is meaningless. BBE renders "cord" for the bowstring; "cord" is well attested in the KJV corpus (e.g. Ecclesiastes 4:12's threefold cord) and fits a bow's string.
- NEW: For, lo, the wicked bend their bow, they make ready their arrow upon the cord, that they may privily shoot at the upright in heart.

## string → band — Mark 7:35
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "exceeding" is meaningless. Wycliffe reads "boond" and Darby/ASV read "band"/"bond" for the ligament of the tongue; "band" is common in the KJV corpus.
- NEW: And straightway his ears were opened, and the band of his tongue was loosed, and he spake plain.

## stripe → hurt — Exodus 21:25
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: "hurt" is a genuinely different, common KJV word that preserves the lex talionis parallelism ("burning for burning, wound for wound, hurt for hurt").
- NEW: Burning for burning, wound for wound, hurt for hurt.

## stripling → youth — I Samuel 17:56
- verdict: KEEP

- NEW: And the king said, Enquire thou whose son the youth is.

## stript → spoiled — Genesis 37:23
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "stripped" is a mere re-inflection of the flagged word (disguised same-lemma swap), which the rules forbid. "Spoiled" is a genuinely different, well-attested KJV word for stripping/plundering someone of clothing or goods.
- NEW: And it came to pass, when Joseph was come unto his brethren, that they spoiled Joseph of his coat, his coat of many colours that was on him;

## striven → contended — Jeremiah 50:24
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR breaks the auxiliary ("thou hast meddle"). Darby renders "contended with Jehovah"; "contended" is common in the KJV corpus and the auxiliary "hast" now agrees.
- NEW: I have laid a snare for thee, and thou art also taken, O Babylon, and thou wast not aware: thou art found, and also caught, because thou hast contended against the Lord.

## strongly → firmly — Ezra 6:3

## struggled → strove — Genesis 25:22
- verdict: REVISE

- NEW: And the children strove together within her; and she said, If it be so, why am I thus? And she went to enquire of the Lord.

## studieth → thinketh — Proverbs 15:28
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "speaketh" duplicates the verb of the next clause's sense and changes the meaning ("heart speaketh" is odd; the heart deliberates before the mouth answers). Wycliffe reads "bithenkith" (bethinks) and YLT "meditateth"; "thinketh" is common in the KJV corpus and preserves the sense of deliberation.
- NEW: The heart of the righteous thinketh to answer: but the mouth of the wicked poureth out evil things.

## studieth → thinketh — Proverbs 24:2
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Same fix as Proverbs 15:28 for consistency and the same rationale (deliberate scheming, not mere speech).
- NEW: For their heart thinketh destruction, and their lips talk of mischief.
