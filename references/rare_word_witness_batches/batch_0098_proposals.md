# Batch 0098 proposals

## succeedest → possessest — Deuteronomy 12:29
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR is nonsensical ("thou mayest them"). Geneva/DRC read "shalt possesse them"; "possessest" is attested in the KJV corpus, keeps the thou/-est inflection, and preserves the sense of coming into possession after conquest.
- NEW: When the Lord thy God shall cut off the nations from before thee, whither thou goest to possess them, and thou possessest them, and dwellest in their land;

## succeedest → possessest — Deuteronomy 19:1
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Same fix as Deuteronomy 12:29, consistent with Geneva/DRC.
- NEW: When the Lord thy God hath cut off the nations, whose land the Lord thy God giveth thee, and thou possessest them, and dwellest in their cities, and in their houses;

## success → speed — Joshua 1:8
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR duplicates "goods" nonsensically. "Speed" is a period idiom for success/prosperity attested in the KJV corpus (2 John 1:10-11 "God speed"), and preserves the sense of prospering.
- NEW: This book of the law shall not depart out of thy mouth; but thou shalt meditate therein day and night, that thou mayest observe to do according to all that is written therein: for then thou shalt make thy way prosperous, and then thou shalt have good speed.

## succourer → helper — Romans 16:2
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "leader" inverts the servant role Paul describes. RWebster/ASV/Darby all render "helper," which is common in the KJV corpus and correctly preserves the sense that she assisted many.
- NEW: That ye receive her in the Lord, as becometh saints, and that ye assist her in whatsoever business she hath need of you: for she hath been a helper of many, and of myself also.

## sue → contend — Matthew 5:40
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "judge" garbles the sense of being taken to court. DRC reads "contend with thee in judgment"; "contend" is common in the KJV corpus and preserves the legal sense.
- NEW: And if any man will contend with thee at the law, and take away thy coat, let him have thy cloke also.

## sufficiently → enough — II Chronicles 30:3

- NEW: For they could not keep it at that time, because the priests had not sanctified themselves enough, neither had the people gathered themselves together to Jerusalem.

## supplant → deceive — Jeremiah 9:4
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "stay" inverts the meaning (supplant/undermine vs. support). Geneva reads "will vse deceite"; "deceive" is common in the KJV corpus and correctly preserves the sense of treacherous undermining among brethren.
- NEW: Take ye heed every one of his neighbour, and trust ye not in any brother: for every brother will utterly deceive, and every neighbour will walk with slanders.

## supplanted → deceived — Genesis 27:36
- verdict: REVISE

- NEW: And he said, Is not he rightly named Jacob? for he hath deceived me these two times: he took away my birthright; and, behold, now he hath taken away my blessing. And he said, Hast thou not reserved a blessing for me?

## supple → cleanse — Ezekiel 16:4

- NEW: And as for thy nativity, in the day thou wast born thy navel was not cut, neither wast thou washed in water to cleanse thee; thou wast not salted at all, nor swaddled at all.

## suretiship → pledges — Proverbs 11:15
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "blow" is nonsensical. Since "surety" already appears earlier in this same verse and may not be duplicated, "pledges" (cf. Genesis 38:17, an attested KJV word for a surety-guarantee) is used instead to preserve the sense of hating to stand guarantee for another's debt.
- NEW: He that is surety for a stranger shall smart for it: and he that hateth pledges is sure.

- NEW: And take heed to yourselves, lest at any time your hearts be overcharged with excess, and drunkenness, and cares of this life, and so that day come upon you unawares.
