# Batch 0099 proposals

## swan → [no swap] — Leviticus 11:18
- verdict: NO-SAFE-SWAP
- rationale: The identity of this unclean bird is genuinely disputed among all witnesses (Darby keeps "swan," ASV has "horned owl," BSB has "osprey," DRC has "porphyrion"); "stork" is already a distinct bird named in the very next verse of this list (Leviticus 11:19), so substituting it here risks misidentifying the species with no reliable evidence either way.
- NEW: (no change proposed — flag for owner review)

## swan → [no swap] — Deuteronomy 14:16
- verdict: NO-SAFE-SWAP
- rationale: Same issue as Leviticus 11:18 — witnesses disagree on the bird's identity (DRC actually lists both "swan" and "stork" as separate birds in this very verse), so replacing "swan" with "stork" would conflate two distinct list items with no reliable evidence for the swap.
- NEW: (no change proposed — flag for owner review)

## swearers → perjured persons — Malachi 3:5
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "causes" is nonsensical. "Perjured persons" is the KJV's own exact phrase (1 Timothy 1:10 "for perjured persons"), so this is a well-attested, genuinely different word preserving the sense of those who swear falsely.
- NEW: And I will come near to you to judgment; and I will be a swift witness against the sorcerers, and against the adulterers, and against perjured persons, and against those that oppress the hireling in his wages, the widow, and the fatherless, and that turn aside the stranger from his right, and fear not me, saith the Lord of hosts.

## sweetly → greedily — Job 24:20
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "uprightly" makes no sense of a worm feeding on a corpse. "Greedily" is attested in the KJV corpus (Ezekiel 22:12, Proverbs 21:26) and preserves the sense of the worm feeding readily/eagerly.
- NEW: The womb shall forget him; the worm shall feed greedily on him; he shall be no more remembered; and wickedness shall be broken as a tree.

## sweetly → gently — Song of Solomon 7:9
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "uprightly" is nonsensical for wine going down. "Gently" is well attested in the KJV corpus and is close to Darby/ASV's "smoothly" (which is itself unattested), preserving the sense of the wine's easy flow.
- NEW: And the roof of thy mouth like the best wine for my beloved, that goeth down gently, causing the lips of those that are asleep to speak.

## sweetsmelling → odour of a sweet smell — Ephesians 5:2
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "smelling savour" alone is awkward. YLT/ASV render "an odour of a sweet smell"; "odour" is attested in the KJV corpus (Leviticus 26:31) and this rewords the compound entirely rather than merely dropping half of it.
- NEW: And walk in love, as Christ also hath loved us, and hath given himself for us an offering and a sacrifice to God for an odour of a sweet smell.

## swerved → erred — I Timothy 1:6
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: CUR's "erred" matches Geneva/Wycliffe/DRC exactly and is common in the KJV corpus.
- NEW: From which some having erred have turned aside unto vain jangling;

## swollen → swelled — Acts 28:6
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "bloated" is not attested in the KJV corpus and reads as a modern word. "Swelled" is attested in the KJV corpus and is a genuinely different form/word from "swollen" preserving the exact same sense.
- NEW: Howbeit they looked when he should have swelled, or fallen down dead suddenly: but after they had looked a great while, and saw no harm come to him, they changed their minds, and said that he was a god.

## swoon → faint — Lamentations 2:11
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "feeble" breaks the grammar (missing a verb). "Faint" as a verb is common in the KJV corpus (Genesis 45:26, Isaiah 40:30) and matches DRC's "fainted away," preserving the sense of collapsing from hunger.
- NEW: Mine eyes do fail with tears, my bowels are troubled, my liver is poured upon the earth, for the destruction of the daughter of my people; because the children and the sucklings faint in the streets of the city.

## swooned → fainted — Lamentations 2:12
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "overwhelmed" loses the specific sense of collapsing from want. "Fainted" is common in the KJV corpus and consistent with the companion verse (2:11), matching DRC's "fainted away."
- NEW: They say to their mothers, Where is corn and wine? when they fainted as the wounded in the streets of the city, when their soul was poured out into their mothers' bosom.

## sycamine → mulberry — Luke 17:6
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR duplicates "tree tree" nonsensically. Tyndale, Geneva, DRC, and RWebster all render "mulberry tree," which is attested in the KJV corpus and preserves the sense of a specific rooted tree being uprooted.
- NEW: And the Lord said, If ye had faith as a grain of mustard seed, ye might say unto this mulberry tree, Be thou plucked up by the root, and be thou planted in the sea; and it should obey you.

## tabering → beating — Nahum 2:7

## tackling → [no swap] — Acts 27:19

- NEW: (no change proposed — likely spurious flag; recommend leaving "tackling")

## taker → usurer — Isaiah 24:2
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR is grammatically broken ("the exact of usury"). "Usurer" is the KJV's own exact word (Exodus 22:25 "thou shalt not be to him as an usurer"), so this rewords the phrase using an attested, genuinely different noun.
- NEW: And it shall be, as with the people, so with the priest; as with the servant, so with his master; as with the maid, so with her mistress; as with the buyer, so with the seller; as with the lender, so with the borrower; as with the usurer, so with him that giveth to usury.

## talkers → speakers — Ezekiel 36:3
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "tongues" shifts the sense from gossiping people to the organ/language itself. Geneva's own rendering "lippes and tongues of men" partly supports an alternate reading, but "speakers" (Darby's choice for the companion Titus 1:10 usage) is common in the KJV corpus and preserves the sense of idle talkers most clearly.
- NEW: Therefore prophesy and say, Thus saith the Lord God; Because they have made you desolate, and swallowed you up on every side, that ye might be a possession unto the residue of the heathen, and ye are taken up in the lips of speakers, and are an infamy of the people:

## talkers → speakers — Titus 1:10
