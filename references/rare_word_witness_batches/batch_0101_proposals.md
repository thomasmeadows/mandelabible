# Batch 0101 proposals

## thankworthy → grace — I Peter 2:19
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: CUR's "grace" is Wycliffe's exact rendering ("this is grace, if...") and YLT uses "gracious"; in the period sense "grace" can mean a commendable/praiseworthy thing, preserving the sense.
- NEW: For this is grace, if a man for conscience toward God endure grief, suffering wrongfully.

## theatre → common place — Acts 19:29
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: The King James agent flagged this as having no safe one-word swap. Geneva/Tyndale avoid the loanword entirely, rendering "the common place"; this is attested by period witnesses and is a genuinely different phrase preserving the sense of a public gathering place.
- NEW: And the whole city was filled with confusion: and having caught Gaius and Aristarchus, men of Macedonia, Paul's companions in travel, they rushed with one accord into the common place.

## theatre → common place — Acts 19:31
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Same fix as Acts 19:29 for consistency, using Geneva's "the Common place."
- NEW: And certain of the chief of Asia, which were his friends, sent unto him, desiring him that he would not adventure himself into the common place.

## thee-ward → toward thee — I Samuel 19:4
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "sore" is nonsensical. RWebster/ASV render "toward thee"; this rewording replaces the compound with an attested, natural phrase preserving the sense of David's good conduct toward Saul.
- NEW: And Jonathan spake good of David unto Saul his father, and said unto him, Let not the king sin against his servant, against David; because he hath not sinned against thee, and because his works have been toward thee very good:

## theft → [no swap] — Exodus 22:3
- verdict: NO-SAFE-SWAP
- rationale: "Theft/thefts" is itself an attested KJV word (Matthew 15:19, Mark 7:22), so this looks like a spurious flag. CUR's "robbery" is also a legally distinct term implying violence, which is not present in this text about a thief caught secretly taking livestock — swapping in "robbery" would misrepresent the offense described.
- NEW: (no change proposed — likely spurious flag; "robbery" would misstate the offense)

- NEW: (no change proposed — likely spurious flag; recommend leaving "thirdly")
