# Rare word witness batch 0102 — proposals

## thronged → choked — Mark 5:24
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "choked" inverts meaning (strangled vs. crowded around). KJV itself uses "press" for this exact scene at Luke 8:45 ("the multitudes throng thee and press thee"); Darby/BSB likewise render "pressed" here.
- NEW: And Jesus went with him; and much people followed him, and pressed him.

## thronged → choked — Luke 8:42
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Same fix as Mark 5:24; "pressed" matches the parallel KJV construction at Luke 8:45 and avoids the meaning-inverting "choked."
- NEW: For he had one only daughter, about twelve years of age, and she lay a dying. But as he went the people pressed him.

## thronging → pressing — Mark 5:31
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "pressing" is grammatical, matches Darby ("pressing on thee") and BSB ("pressing in on You"), and is the natural companion to the Mark 5:24/Luke 8:42 fix above (cf. KJV Luke 8:45 "throng...and press").
- NEW: And his disciples said unto him, Thou seest the multitude pressing thee, and sayest thou, Who touched me?

## thunderbolts → coals — Psalms 78:48
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "coals" changes the referent from a sky-fire (lightning) to hearth-coals. "Lightnings" is well-attested in KJV (Exodus 19:16, Psalm 135:7, Nahum 2:4) and matches BSB "bolts of lightning" / YLT "burning flames."
- NEW: He gave up their cattle also to the hail, and their flocks to hot lightnings.

## tie → bound — I Samuel 6:7
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "bound" breaks the imperative series (take...tie...bring). "Fasten" preserves the imperative and avoids duplicating "yoke" already in the verse.
- NEW: Now therefore make a new cart, and take two milch kine, on which there hath come no yoke, and fasten the kine to the cart, and bring their calves home from them:

## tie → bound — Proverbs 6:21
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Same fix as I Samuel 6:7 for consistency; avoids duplicating "Bind" already used at the start of the verse.
- NEW: Bind them continually upon thine heart, and fasten them about thy neck.

## tile → brick — Ezekiel 4:1
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "brick" is well-attested in KJV (Genesis 11:3, Exodus 1:14) and matches YLT, Geneva1599, Darby, and BSB.
- NEW: Thou also, son of man, take thee a brick, and lay it before thee, and pourtray upon it the city, even Jerusalem:

## tiling → roof — Luke 5:19
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "roof" is distinct from "housetop" earlier in the verse (no duplication) and matches DRC/BBE.
- NEW: And when they could not find by what way they might bring him in because of the multitude, they went upon the housetop, and let him down through the roof with his couch into the midst before Jesus.

## tiller → serve — Genesis 4:2
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "serve" is the wrong part of speech (verb for a noun slot). "Husbandman" is KJV-attested (Genesis 9:20 "an husbandman," 2 Timothy 2:6, James 5:7), matches DRC/Darby, and preserves the KJV convention "an husbandman."
- NEW: And she again bare his brother Abel. And Abel was a keeper of sheep, but Cain was an husbandman of the ground.

## tire → beauty — Ezekiel 24:17
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "beauty" turns a headdress into an abstraction. "Bonnet" is the KJV's own word for this kind of headdress (Exodus 28:40, Leviticus 8:13) and matches YLT here.
- NEW: Forbear to cry, make no mourning for the dead, bind the bonnet of thine head upon thee, and put on thy shoes upon thy feet, and cover not thy lips, and eat not the bread of men.

## tired → adorned — II Kings 9:30
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "adorned" matches Webster and DRC and is well-attested (1 Peter 3:5, Titus 2:10).
- NEW: And when Jehu was come to Jezreel, Jezebel heard of it; and she painted her face, and adorned her head, and looked out at a window.

## tires → attire — Isaiah 3:18
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "attire" is well-attested in KJV (Jeremiah 2:32, Proverbs 7:10) and does not invert the sense of ornament being removed.
- NEW: In that day the Lord will take away the bravery of their tinkling ornaments about their feet, and their cauls, and their round attire like the moon,

## tires → attire — Ezekiel 24:23
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: For consistency with the Ezekiel 24:17 fix (same headdress in the same book), "bonnets" matches YLT exactly and is KJV-attested (Exodus 28:40, Leviticus 8:13), sharper than the more generic "attire."
- NEW: And your bonnets shall be upon your heads, and your shoes upon your feet: ye shall not mourn nor weep; but ye shall pine away for your iniquities, and mourn one toward another.

## tittle → point — Matthew 5:18
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "point" does not invert the sense (a minute detail of the law will not pass away); it is a well-attested KJV word and no period witness offers a genuinely different, non-circular alternative (Geneva/Tyndale's "title" is essentially the same word as "tittle").
- NEW: For verily I say unto you, Till heaven and earth pass, one jot or one point shall in no wise pass from the law, till all be fulfilled.

## tittle → point — Luke 16:17
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Same reasoning as Matthew 5:18.
- NEW: And it is easier for heaven and earth to pass, than one point of the law to fail.

## torch → lamp — Zechariah 12:6
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "lamp" loses the firebrand-thrust-into-sheaf imagery. "Firebrand" is KJV-attested (Judges 15:4-5, Proverbs 26:18) and matches Geneva1599/DRC exactly.
- NEW: In that day will I make the governors of Judah like an hearth of fire among the wood, and like a firebrand of fire in a sheaf; and they shall devour all the people round about, on the right hand and on the left: and Jerusalem shall be inhabited again in her own place, even in Jerusalem.

## tormentors → executioners — Matthew 18:34
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "executioners" inverts the sense — a dead debtor cannot pay. "Keepers" (i.e. jailers, cf. Tyndale "iaylers," KJV's own "keeper of the prison" idiom) is well-attested (Matthew 28:4, Acts 5:23) and preserves the meaning of imprisonment until payment.
- NEW: And his lord was wroth, and delivered him to the keepers, till he should pay all that was due unto him.

## tortoise → covered — Leviticus 11:29
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "covered" is an adjective where a creature-noun is required. "Frog" matches Geneva1599 exactly and avoids duplicating "lizard," which the KJV itself uses in the very next verse (11:30).
- NEW: These also shall be unclean unto you among the creeping things that creep upon the earth; the weasel, and the mouse, and the frog after his kind,

## tortured → afflicted — Hebrews 11:35
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "afflicted" is broad but does not invert the sense of suffering, and is far more KJV-common than the period witnesses' "racked."
- NEW: Women received their dead raised to life again: and others were afflicted, not accepting deliverance; that they might obtain a better resurrection:

## toss → themselves — Isaiah 22:18
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "themselves" (a pronoun) replaces a verb, producing nonsense. "Roll" is KJV-attested (Joshua 10:18) and matches Geneva1599's "rolle and turne" and Darby/BSB.
- NEW: He will surely violently turn and roll thee like a ball into a large country: there shalt thou die, and there the chariots of thy glory shall be the shame of thy lord's house.

## toss → themselves — Jeremiah 5:22
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR produces "themselves themselves," a broken duplicate. "Rage" matches Geneva1599 exactly ("though the waues thereof rage") and reads grammatically.
- NEW: Fear ye not me? saith the Lord: will ye not tremble at my presence, which have placed the sand for the bound of the sea by a perpetual decree, that it cannot pass it: and though the waves thereof rage, yet can they not prevail; though they roar, yet can they not pass over it?

## tossings → tossing — Job 7:4
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR merely singularizes the flagged word (forbidden re-inflection). "Turning" is a genuinely different, KJV-attested word (James 1:17) that preserves the restless-motion sense, echoed by BSB's "toss and turn."
- NEW: When I lie down, I say, When shall I arise, and the night be gone? and I am full of turning to and fro unto the dawning of the day.

## tottering → casting — Psalms 62:3
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "casting" is nonsensical for a fence. "Shaken" is KJV-attested (Matthew 24:29, Hebrews 12:26-27), echoes Geneva1599's rendering of this verse ("a wall shaken"), and avoids duplicating "bowing" earlier in the verse.
- NEW: How long will ye imagine mischief against a man? ye shall be slain all of you: as a bowing wall shall ye be, and as a shaken fence.

## towel → napkin — John 13:4
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "napkin" is well-attested in KJV (John 11:44, 20:7, Luke 19:20) for a cloth of this kind.
- NEW: He riseth from supper, and laid aside his garments; and took a napkin, and girded himself.
