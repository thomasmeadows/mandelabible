# Rare word witness batch 0103 — proposals

## towel → napkin — John 13:5
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: Same reasoning as John 13:4 in batch 0102; "napkin" is well-attested KJV usage.
- NEW: After that he poureth water into a bason, and began to wash the disciples' feet, and to wipe them with the napkin wherewith he was girded.

## townclerk → scribe — Acts 19:35
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: Wycliffe attests "scribe" for this exact verse ("the scribe hadde ceessid the puple"), giving direct period justification despite the modern association of "scribe" with Jewish teachers of the law.
- NEW: And when the scribe had appeased the people, he said, Ye men of Ephesus, what man is there that knoweth not how that the city of the Ephesians is a worshipper of the great goddess Diana, and of the image which fell down from Jupiter?

## traffickers → merchants — Isaiah 23:8
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR duplicates "merchants" already in the verse. "Chapmen" is KJV-attested (2 Chronicles 9:14 "chapmen and merchants") and matches Geneva1599 exactly, avoiding the duplicate.
- NEW: Who hath taken this counsel against Tyre, the crowning city, whose merchants are princes, whose chapmen are the honourable of the earth?

## traitor → betrayer — Luke 6:16
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: The noun "betrayer" is not attested in the KJV corpus (a new rare word). Rephrasing as a relative clause with the well-attested verb "betrayed" (rule: agent-noun → relative clause) preserves the meaning without introducing another rarity.
- NEW: And Judas the brother of James, and Judas Iscariot, which also betrayed him.

## tranquillity → quietness — Daniel 4:27
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: "quietness" is well-attested (Ecclesiastes 4:6, Isaiah 30:15, 1 Thessalonians 4:11) and matches the sense of extended peace/prosperity.
- NEW: Wherefore, O king, let my counsel be acceptable unto thee, and break off thy sins by righteousness, and thine iniquities by shewing mercy to the poor; if it may be a lengthening of thy quietness.

## transferred → transformed — I Corinthians 4:6
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "transformed" implies a change of nature, not Paul's figurative application of the argument to himself. Geneva1599 attests "applied" directly in this very verse ("I haue figuratiuely applied vnto mine owne selfe and Apollos").
- NEW: And these things, brethren, I have in a figure applied to myself and to Apollos for your sakes; that ye might learn in us not to think of men above that which is written, that no one of you be puffed up for one against another.

## transfigured → transformed — Matthew 17:2
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: "transformed" is KJV-attested (Romans 12:2, 2 Corinthians 3:18) and does not invert the meaning.
- NEW: And was transformed before them: and his face did shine as the sun, and his raiment was white as the light.

## transfigured → transformed — Mark 9:2
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: Same as Matthew 17:2.
- NEW: And after six days Jesus taketh with him Peter, and James, and John, and leadeth them up into an high mountain apart by themselves: and he was transformed before them.

## transforming → changing — II Corinthians 11:13
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "changing" is vague; "fashioning" (ASV) is KJV-attested (Philippians 3:21 "fashioned like unto his glorious body") and better conveys the disguise sense of the Greek.
- NEW: For such are false apostles, deceitful workers, fashioning themselves into the apostles of Christ.

## translate → cause — II Samuel 3:10
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "cause" leaves the sentence without a verb object ("to cause the kingdom from..."). "Remove" matches Geneva1599 exactly and is grammatical.
- NEW: To remove the kingdom from the house of Saul, and to set up the throne of David over Israel and over Judah, from Dan even to Beer-sheba.

## translation → change — Hebrews 11:5
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: "change" is KJV-attested in the same sense (Job 14:14 "till my change come") and fits the euphemism for Enoch's being taken up.
- NEW: By faith Enoch was translated that he should not see death; and was not found, because God had translated him: for before his change he had this testimony, that he pleased God.

## transparent → clear — Revelation 21:21
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: "clear" is KJV-attested (Revelation 21:11, Song of Solomon 6:10) and matches BBE/BSB.
- NEW: And the twelve gates were twelve pearls; every several gate was of one pearl: and the street of the city was pure gold, as it were clear glass.

## travel → journey — Acts 19:29
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "in journey" is missing a needed possessive/gerund. "Journeying" is KJV-attested (Numbers 10:2, 10:6) and fixes the grammar.
- NEW: And the whole city was filled with confusion: and having caught Gaius and Aristarchus, men of Macedonia, Paul's companions in journeying, they rushed with one accord into the theatre.

## travel → journey — II Corinthians 8:19
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: "to journey with us" is grammatical as a verb and "journey" is KJV-attested as both noun and verb (Genesis 35:5, Numbers 9:17); matches Geneva1599's concept.
- NEW: And not that only, but who was also chosen of the churches to journey with us with this grace, which is administered by us to the glory of the same Lord, and declaration of your ready mind:

## travelled → passed — Acts 11:19
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: "passed" is common KJV usage and preserves the sense; matches Darby's "passed through."
- NEW: Now they which were scattered abroad upon the persecution that arose about Stephen passed as far as Phenice, and Cyprus, and Antioch, preaching the word to none but unto the Jews only.

## traveller → way — II Samuel 12:4
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "way" is nonsensical as the subject ("there came a way"). "Stranger" matches Geneva1599/DRC and avoids duplicating "wayfaring man" later in the same verse.
- NEW: And there came a stranger unto the rich man, and he spared to take of his own flock and of his own herd, to dress for the wayfaring man that was come unto him; but took the poor man's lamb, and dressed it for the man that was come to him.

## traveller → way — Job 31:32
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "way" is nonsensical as the object of "opened my doors." "Wayfaring man" is KJV-attested (Isaiah 33:8, Judges 19:17) and distinct from "stranger" already in the verse, preserving the poetic parallelism.
- NEW: The stranger did not lodge in the street: but I opened my doors to the wayfaring man.

## travellers → needs — Judges 5:6
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "needs" is nonsensical as the subject. "Wayfaring men" is KJV-attested and fits; see cross-batch note — this verse also has "unoccupied" flagged in batch 0105, resolved together as "forsaken... wayfaring men."
- NEW: In the days of Shamgar the son of Anath, in the days of Jael, the highways were unoccupied, and the wayfaring men walked through byways.

## travelleth → walketh — Proverbs 6:11
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "walketh" is bland and loses the ambush imagery. Four witnesses (ASV, ACV, Darby, BSB) independently converge on "robber"/"plunderer" for the underlying sense; ASV's exact phrasing is adopted.
- NEW: So shall thy poverty come as a robber, and thy want as an armed man.

## travelleth → walketh — Proverbs 24:34
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Same fix as Proverbs 6:11 (parallel verse).
- NEW: So shall thy poverty come as a robber; and thy want as an armed man.

## traversing → winding — Jeremiah 2:23
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: "winding" matches YLT exactly and preserves the erratic-motion imagery.
- NEW: How canst thou say, I am not polluted, I have not gone after Baalim? see thy way in the valley, know what thou hast done: thou art a swift dromedary winding her ways;

## treachery → deceit — II Kings 9:23
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: "deceit" matches YLT exactly and is well-attested; not a meaning inversion.
- NEW: And Joram turned his hands, and fled, and said to Ahaziah, There is deceit, O Ahaziah.

## treader → tread — Amos 9:13
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "tread" is the wrong part of speech in a noun slot. Rephrasing as a relative clause ("he that treadeth grapes") restores grammar while keeping the same verb-lemma, per the agent-noun-to-relative-clause rule.
- NEW: Behold, the days come, saith the Lord, that the plowman shall overtake the reaper, and he that treadeth grapes him that soweth seed; and the mountains shall drop sweet wine, and all the hills shall melt.

## treaders → archers — Isaiah 16:10
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "archers" is a clear error (unrelated to wine treading). Dropping the agent-noun and using ASV's verb-only structure ("none shall tread out wine") preserves the sense without the flagged word.
- NEW: And gladness is taken away, and joy out of the plentiful field; and in the vineyards there shall be no singing, neither shall there be shouting: none shall tread out wine in their presses; I have made their vintage shouting to cease.

## treasurer → profitable — Ezra 1:8
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "profitable" is the wrong part of speech. "Steward" is well-attested KJV usage (Genesis 15:2, Luke 16:1) and matches the fix used at Isaiah 22:15 below, kept consistent within the batch.
- NEW: Even those did Cyrus king of Persia bring forth by the hand of Mithredath the steward, and numbered them unto Sheshbazzar, the prince of Judah.

## treasurer → profitable — Isaiah 22:15
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Same fix as Ezra 1:8; matches YLT/Darby's "steward" for this verse directly.
- NEW: Thus saith the Lord God of hosts, Go, get thee unto this steward, even unto Shebna, which is over the house, and say,

## treatise → word — Acts 1:1
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "word" is too minimal for a written narrative. "Story" is KJV-attested in exactly this sense (2 Chronicles 13:22, 24:27 "story of the book of the kings") and preserves the meaning of a written account.
- NEW: The former story have I made, O Theophilus, of all that Jesus began both to do and teach,
