# Rare word witness batch 0104 — proposals

## tributary → tribute — Lamentations 1:1
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "become tribute" is grammatically odd (noun where a predicate is needed). "Servant" conveys the same subjugation sense idiomatically and is well-attested.
- NEW: How doth the city sit solitary, that was full of people! how is she become as a widow! she that was great among the nations, and princess among the provinces, how is she become a servant!

## trickleth → poureth — Lamentations 3:49
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: "poureth" matches Darby/ASV/ACV exactly.
- NEW: Mine eye poureth down, and ceaseth not, without any intermission,

## trimmed → committed — II Samuel 19:24
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "committed his beard" is nonsensical. The verse already uses "dressed" (feet) and "washed" (clothes), so a third distinct verb is needed: "clipped" avoids duplication and is echoed by Wycliffe's "berd vnclippid."
- NEW: And Mephibosheth the son of Saul came down to meet the king, and had neither dressed his feet, nor clipped his beard, nor washed his clothes, from the day the king departed until the day he came again in peace.

## trimmed → committed — Matthew 25:7
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "committed their lamps" is nonsensical. "Prepared" matches Tyndale exactly and is common KJV usage.
- NEW: Then all those virgins arose, and prepared their lamps.

## trimmest → goest — Jeremiah 2:33
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "goest" loses the sense of adorning/making ready oneself to seek love. "Preparest" matches Geneva1599's "doest thou prepare thy way" and keeps the -est inflection.
- NEW: Why preparest thou thy way to seek love? therefore hast thou also taught the wicked ones thy ways.

## troubledst → troubled — Ezekiel 32:2
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR merely drops the -st ending (forbidden re-inflection of the same word). "Stirredst" is a genuinely different, KJV-attested lemma ("stir") in the correct second-person-singular past form, and avoids duplicating "fouledst" later in the verse.
- NEW: Son of man, take up a lamentation for Pharaoh king of Egypt, and say unto him, Thou art like a young lion of the nations, and thou art as a whale in the seas: and thou camest forth with thy rivers, and stirredst the waters with thy feet, and fouledst their rivers.

## troubler → trouble — I Chronicles 2:7
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "the trouble of Israel" is a weak epithet. Rephrasing as a relative clause, "who troubled Israel," matches Geneva1599/DRC exactly.
- NEW: And the sons of Carmi; Achar, who troubled Israel, who transgressed in the thing accursed.

## troublous → troubled — Daniel 9:25
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: "troubled" is common KJV usage and does not invert the sense of "times of trouble."
- NEW: Know therefore and understand, that from the going forth of the commandment to restore and to build Jerusalem unto the Messiah the Prince shall be seven weeks, and threescore and two weeks: the street shall be built again, and the wall, even in troubled times.

## trough → cistern — Genesis 24:20
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: "cistern" is KJV-attested (2 Kings 18:31, Jeremiah 2:13, Proverbs 5:15) and remains a plausible water-vessel for camels to drink from; not a meaning inversion.
- NEW: And she hasted, and emptied her pitcher into the cistern, and ran again unto the well to draw water, and drew for all his camels.

## troughs → galleries — Genesis 30:38
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "galleries" is a clear error (architectural feature, not a water vessel). "Cisterns" matches the Genesis 24:20 fix above for consistency.
- NEW: And he set the rods which he had pilled before the flocks in the gutters in the watering cisterns when the flocks came to drink, that they should conceive when they came to drink.

## troughs → galleries — Exodus 2:16
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Same fix as Genesis 30:38.
- NEW: Now the priest of Midian had seven daughters: and they came and drew water, and filled the cisterns to water their father's flock.

## trow → suppose — Luke 17:9
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: "suppose" matches Webster and is common KJV usage.
- NEW: Doth he thank that servant because he did the things that were commanded him? I suppose not.

## trucebreakers → covenantbreakers — II Timothy 3:3
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: "covenantbreakers" is itself a KJV word (Romans 1:31, same list-style vice catalogue) and is close enough in sense to "trucebreakers" without inverting meaning.
- NEW: Without natural affection, covenantbreakers, false accusers, incontinent, fierce, despisers of those that are good,

## trump → trumpet — I Corinthians 15:52
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR creates a duplicate ("at the last trumpet: for the trumpet shall sound"). "Horn" is a distinct, attested term for the same instrument (cf. rams'-horn trumpets, Joshua 6) and avoids the repetition; note this verse also has "twinkling" flagged below — combine both fixes.
- NEW: In a moment, in the wink of an eye, at the last horn: for the trumpet shall sound, and the dead shall be raised incorruptible, and we shall be changed.

## trump → trumpet — I Thessalonians 4:16
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: No duplicate risk here (only one occurrence); matches Geneva1599/RWebster exactly.
- NEW: For the Lord himself shall descend from heaven with a shout, with the voice of the archangel, and with the trumpet of God: and the dead in Christ shall rise first:

## trusty → stedfast — Job 12:20
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: "stedfast" matches YLT exactly and is a reasonable synonym for reliable counselors.
- NEW: He removeth away the speech of the stedfast, and taketh away the understanding of the aged.

## tumbled → turned — Judges 7:13
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "turned into" risks being read as transformation rather than rolling motion. "Rolled" matches Webster/RWebster exactly and is well-attested (Joshua 10:18, Genesis 29:3).
- NEW: And when Gideon was come, behold, there was a man that told a dream unto his fellow, and said, Behold, I dreamed a dream, and, lo, a cake of barley bread rolled into the host of Midian, and came unto a tent, and smote it that it fell, and overturned it, that the tent lay along.

## turtle → turtledove — Song of Solomon 2:12
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: "turtledove" is well-attested (Leviticus 1:14) and avoids modern confusion with the reptile; matches RWebster/Darby/ASV.
- NEW: The flowers appear on the earth; the time of the singing of birds is come, and the voice of the turtledove is heard in our land;

## turtle → turtledove — Jeremiah 8:7
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: Same reasoning as Song of Solomon 2:12.
- NEW: Yea, the stork in the heaven knoweth her appointed times; and the turtledove and the crane and the swallow observe the time of their coming; but my people know not the judgment of the Lord.

## tutors → stewards — Galatians 4:2
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: "stewards" matches YLT exactly and is well-attested (Genesis 15:2, Luke 16:1, 1 Corinthians 4:1-2).
- NEW: But is under stewards and governors until the time appointed of the father.

## twigs → branches — Ezekiel 17:4
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: No duplication risk in this verse; "branches" is well-attested and does not invert the sense of cropping new growth.
- NEW: He cropped off the top of his young branches, and carried it into a land of traffick; he set it in a city of merchants.

## twigs → branches — Ezekiel 17:22
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Unlike Ezekiel 17:4, this verse already uses "highest branch" earlier — "branches" would duplicate the root word. "Boughs" is KJV-attested (Genesis 49:22, Judges 9:48) and avoids the duplication.
- NEW: Thus saith the Lord God; I will also take of the highest branch of the high cedar, and will set it; I will crop off from the top of his young boughs a tender one, and will plant it upon an high mountain and eminent:

## twinkling → wink — I Corinthians 15:52
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: "wink of an eye" is a reasonable idiom for an instant and does not invert the meaning; combine with the "horn" fix above for the full verse.
- NEW: In a moment, in the wink of an eye, at the last horn: for the trumpet shall sound, and the dead shall be raised incorruptible, and we shall be changed.

## twofold → double — Matthew 23:15
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: "double more" is an awkward but non-inverting rendering of the same degree-idiom as the original "twofold more"; "double" is extremely common KJV usage.
- NEW: Woe unto you, scribes and Pharisees, hypocrites! for ye compass sea and land to make one proselyte, and when he is made, ye make him double more the child of hell than yourselves.

## unaccustomed → seed — Jeremiah 31:18
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "seed to the yoke" is nonsensical. "Not taught to the yoke" matches YLT closely with the well-attested verb "taught."
- NEW: I have surely heard Ephraim bemoaning himself thus; Thou hast chastised me, and I was chastised, as a bullock not taught to the yoke: turn thou me, and I shall be turned; for thou art the Lord my God.

## unadvisedly → rashly — Psalms 106:33
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: "rashly" matches BSB and is KJV-attested (Ecclesiastes 5:2, Acts 19:36); not a meaning inversion.
- NEW: Because they provoked his spirit, so that he spake rashly with his lips.

## unblameable → blameless — Colossians 1:22
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: "blameless" is well-attested (Philippians 2:15, 3:6). Cross-batch note: this verse also has "unreproveable" flagged in batch 0106 — resolved there as "without fault" to avoid duplicating "blameless" twice in one verse.
- NEW: In the body of his flesh through death, to present you holy and blameless and without fault in his sight:

## unblameable → blameless — I Thessalonians 3:13
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: No duplication risk in this verse; "blameless" is well-attested.
- NEW: To the end he may stablish your hearts blameless in holiness before God, even our Father, at the coming of our Lord Jesus Christ with all his saints.

## unblameably → blamelessly — I Thessalonians 2:10
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: "blamelessly" matches YLT/Darby exactly and is a natural adverbial form of the well-attested "blameless."
- NEW: Ye are witnesses, and God also, how holily and justly and blamelessly we behaved ourselves among you that believe:
