# Rare word witness batch 0105 — proposals

## uncertain → appear — I Corinthians 14:8
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "an appear sound" is ungrammatical (verb where adjective needed). "Doubtful" is KJV-attested (Luke 12:29, Romans 14:1) and fixes both grammar and the a/an agreement.
- NEW: For if the trumpet give a doubtful sound, who shall prepare himself to the battle?

## uncertain → appear — I Timothy 6:17
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Same fix as I Corinthians 14:8 for consistency.
- NEW: Charge them that are rich in this world, that they be not highminded, nor trust in doubtful riches, but in the living God, who giveth us richly all things to enjoy;

## uncertainly → vainly — I Corinthians 9:26
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: "vainly" is a reasonable adverbial rendering of the aimless-running sense, echoed by ACV/BSB "aimlessly"; not a meaning inversion.
- NEW: I therefore so run, not as vainly; so fight I, not as one that beateth the air:

## unchangeable → everlasting — Hebrews 7:24
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: "everlasting" matches Tyndale and DRC exactly.
- NEW: But this man, because he continueth ever, hath an everlasting priesthood.

## unclothed → stripped — II Corinthians 5:4
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: "stripped" is well-attested (Genesis 37:23, Matthew 27:28) and preserves the sense of being without a body.
- NEW: For we that are in this tabernacle do groan, being burdened: not for that we would be stripped, but clothed upon, that mortality might be swallowed up of life.

## uncomely → unseemly — I Corinthians 7:36
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Using "unseemly" here is circular — "unseemly" is itself flagged as a rare word elsewhere in this batch set (Romans 1:27, I Corinthians 13:5 in batch 0106). Rephrasing with the common KJV idiom "do wrong" avoids the circularity.
- NEW: But if any man think that he doeth wrong toward his virgin, if she pass the flower of her age, and need so require, let him do what he will, he sinneth not: let them marry.

## uncomely → unseemly — I Corinthians 12:23
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Same circularity problem as I Corinthians 7:36. "Unhonest" is directly attested by both Tyndale and Geneva1599 for this exact verse.
- NEW: And those members of the body, which we think to be less honourable, upon these we bestow more abundant honour; and our unhonest parts have more abundant comeliness.

## uncondemned → untried — Acts 16:37
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: "untried" (not yet tried in court) is a reasonable, non-inverting rendering matching BSB's "without a trial"; no period witness offers a genuinely different single word.
- NEW: But Paul said unto them, They have beaten us openly untried, being Romans, and have cast us into prison; and now do they thrust us out privily? nay verily; but let them come themselves and fetch us out.

## uncondemned → untried — Acts 22:25
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: Same reasoning as Acts 16:37.
- NEW: And as they bound him with thongs, Paul said unto the centurion that stood by, Is it lawful for you to scourge a man that is a Roman, and untried?

## unequal → equal — Ezekiel 18:25
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "equal" inverts the meaning — the verse is a rhetorical accusation ("are not your ways unequal/unjust?"), and CUR turns it into agreement. "Unjust" is well-attested (Luke 16:10, Romans 3:5) and matches BSB, preserving the accusation.
- NEW: Yet ye say, The way of the Lord is not equal. Hear now, O house of Israel; Is not my way equal? are not your ways unjust?
