# Rare word witness batch 0106 — proposals

## unprofitableness → vanity — Hebrews 7:18
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: "vanity" (KJV's common word for futility/worthlessness, as throughout Ecclesiastes) overlaps substantially with "unprofitableness" and does not invert the sense.
- NEW: For there is verily a disannulling of the commandment going before for the weakness and vanity thereof.

## unquenchable → never — Matthew 3:12
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "never fire" is ungrammatical (adverb for adjective). Rephrasing as "fire that never shall be quenched" matches Tyndale and Geneva1599 exactly.
- NEW: Whose fan is in his hand, and he will throughly purge his floor, and gather his wheat into the garner; but he will burn up the chaff with fire that never shall be quenched.

## unquenchable → never — Luke 3:17
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Same fix as Matthew 3:12, matching Tyndale/Geneva1599 for this verse.
- NEW: Whose fan is in his hand, and he will throughly purge his floor, and will gather the wheat into his garner; but the chaff he will burn with fire that never shall be quenched.

## unreasonable → harm — Acts 25:27
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "it seemeth to me harm" is ungrammatical. Wycliffe attests "with out resoun" for this exact verse; "without reason" restores sense and grammar with direct period support.
- NEW: For it seemeth to me without reason to send a prisoner, and not withal to signify the crimes laid against him.

## unreasonable → harm — II Thessalonians 3:2
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "harm and wicked men" awkwardly pairs a thing with persons. "Bad" (Darby) is a simple, well-attested adjective that fits the person-slot cleanly.
- NEW: And that we may be delivered from bad and wicked men: for all men have not faith.

## unrebukeable → blameless — I Timothy 6:14
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: No duplication risk in this verse; "blameless" is well-attested.
- NEW: That thou keep this commandment without spot, blameless, until the appearing of our Lord Jesus Christ:

## unreproveable → blameless — Colossians 1:22
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Cross-batch dependency — batch 0104 already revises "unblameable" to "blameless" in this same verse; using "blameless" again here would duplicate it. "Without fault" matches Geneva1599 exactly and avoids the repetition.
- NEW: In the body of his flesh through death, to present you holy and blameless and without fault in his sight:

## unrighteously → wickedly — Deuteronomy 25:16
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: "wickedly" matches Geneva1599 exactly.
- NEW: For all that do such things, and all that do wickedly, are an abomination unto the Lord thy God.

## unripe → grape — Job 15:33
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR duplicates "grape grape" (nonsensical). "Sour" is directly attested by Geneva1599 ("her sower grape") and is well-attested KJV vocabulary (Jeremiah 31:29-30, Ezekiel 18:2).
- NEW: He shall shake off his sour grape as the vine, and shall cast off his flower as the olive.

## unsatiable → full — Ezekiel 16:28
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "full" directly contradicts the following clause "yet couldest not be satisfied" — a meaning inversion. "Not satisfied" restores internal consistency, echoing DRC's "not yet satisfied."
- NEW: Thou hast played the whore also with the Assyrians, because thou wast not satisfied; yea, thou hast played the harlot with them, and yet couldest not be satisfied.

## unsavoury → froward — II Samuel 22:27
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR duplicates "froward...froward." "Contrary" matches Darby and is well-attested, preserving the contrastive wordplay without repeating the word.
- NEW: With the pure thou wilt shew thyself pure; and with the froward thou wilt shew thyself contrary.

## unsavoury → froward — Job 6:6
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "froward" is a category error when applied to food. Rephrasing with the well-attested KJV word "savour" (Matthew 5:13) matches ASV's structure and preserves sense.
- NEW: Can that which hath no savour be eaten without salt? or is there any taste in the white of an egg?

## unseemly → uncomely — Romans 1:27
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "uncomely" is itself flagged as a rare word elsewhere in this batch set (I Corinthians 7:36, 12:23 in batch 0105) — using it here would be circular. "Filthy" matches Tyndale/Geneva1599's "filthiness" and is well-attested (Zechariah 3:3-4, Revelation 22:11).
- NEW: And likewise also the men, leaving the natural use of the woman, burned in their lust one toward another; men with men working that which is filthy, and receiving in themselves that recompence of their error which was meet.

## unseemly → uncomely — I Corinthians 13:5
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Same circularity problem as Romans 1:27. Tyndale attests "dishonestly" directly for this verse.
- NEW: Doth not behave itself dishonestly, seeketh not her own, is not easily provoked, thinketh no evil;

## unshod → barefoot — Jeremiah 2:25
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: "barefoot" is well-attested (2 Samuel 15:30, Isaiah 20:2-4) and preserves the meaning exactly.
- NEW: Withhold thy foot from being barefoot, and thy throat from thirst: but thou saidst, There is no hope: no; for I have loved strangers, and after them will I go.

## unskilful → unlearned — Hebrews 5:13
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: "unlearned" is well-attested (1 Corinthians 14:16, 2 Timothy 2:23, 2 Peter 3:16) and preserves the sense of inexperience.
- NEW: For every one that useth milk is unlearned in the word of righteousness: for he is a babe.

## unspotted → undefiled — James 1:27
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR duplicates "undefiled," already used earlier in the same verse ("Pure religion and undefiled before God"). "Clean" is well-attested KJV vocabulary and avoids all duplication ("Pure"/"undefiled" already used).
- NEW: Pure religion and undefiled before God and the Father is this, To visit the fatherless and widows in their affliction, and to keep himself clean from the world.

## unstopped → opened — Isaiah 35:5
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR duplicates "opened," used for the eyes in the same verse, losing the KJV's deliberate variation. Shifting to "shall hear" preserves the sense (deaf ears unstopped = made able to hear) without repetition.
- NEW: Then the eyes of the blind shall be opened, and the ears of the deaf shall hear.

## untaken → away — II Corinthians 3:14
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR duplicates "away away" (nonsensical). Rephrasing with simple negation, "not taken away," restores grammar without introducing a new word.
- NEW: But their minds were blinded: for until this day remaineth the same vail, not taken away, in the reading of the old testament; which vail is done away in Christ.

## unthankful → ungrateful — Luke 6:35
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "ungrateful" is not attested in any pre-1611 witness here; both Tyndale and Geneva1599 use "unkind" directly for this verse ("he is kynde vnto the vnkynde").
- NEW: But love ye your enemies, and do good, and lend, hoping for nothing again; and your reward shall be great, and ye shall be the children of the Highest: for he is kind unto the unkind and to the evil.

## unthankful → ungrateful — II Timothy 3:2
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Same period-attestation fix as Luke 6:35 ("unkind"), applied for consistency.
- NEW: For men shall be lovers of their own selves, covetous, boasters, proud, blasphemers, disobedient to parents, unkind, unholy,

## untoward → perverse — Acts 2:40
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: "perverse" matches Webster, RWebster, YLT, Darby, and DRC exactly.
- NEW: And with many other words did he testify and exhort, saying, Save yourselves from this perverse generation.

## unweighed → untold — I Kings 7:47
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: "untold" (innumerable) reasonably conveys the sense of an unmeasured quantity and fits the following clause "because they were exceeding many"; not a meaning inversion.
- NEW: And Solomon left all the vessels untold, because they were exceeding many: neither was the weight of the brass found out.

## unworthily → irreverently — I Corinthians 11:27
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: "irreverently" narrows but does not invert "unworthily"; no period witness offers an alternative (all keep "unworthily").
- NEW: Wherefore whosoever shall eat this bread, and drink this cup of the Lord, irreverently, shall be guilty of the body and blood of the Lord.

## unworthily → irreverently — I Corinthians 11:29
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: Same reasoning as I Corinthians 11:27.
- NEW: For he that eateth and drinketh irreverently, eateth and drinketh damnation to himself, not discerning the Lord's body.

## unworthy → worthy — Acts 13:46
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "worthy" is a direct meaning inversion — it flips the Jews' rejection of the gospel into acceptance of it, contradicting the following clause "we turn to the Gentiles." Simple negation "not worthy" restores the correct sense without needing the flagged compound.
- NEW: Then Paul and Barnabas waxed bold, and said, It was necessary that the word of God should first have been spoken to you: but seeing ye put it from you, and judge yourselves not worthy of everlasting life, lo, we turn to the Gentiles.

## unworthy → none — I Corinthians 6:2
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "are ye none to judge" is ungrammatical. Simple negation "not worthy" restores grammar and sense.
- NEW: Do ye not know that the saints shall judge the world? and if the world shall be judged by you, are ye not worthy to judge the smallest matters?

## upbraid → reproach — Judges 8:15
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: "reproach" matches YLT/UKJV exactly and is extremely well-attested.
- NEW: And he came unto the men of Succoth, and said, Behold Zebah and Zalmunna, with whom ye did reproach me, saying, Are the hands of Zebah and Zalmunna now in thine hand, that we should give bread unto thy men that are weary?

## upbraid → reproach — Matthew 11:20
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: "reproach" matches YLT/Darby/UKJV exactly.
- NEW: Then began he to reproach the cities wherein most of his mighty works were done, because they repented not:

## upbraided → reproached — Mark 16:14
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: "reproached" matches Geneva1599/YLT exactly.
- NEW: Afterward he appeared unto the eleven as they sat at meat, and reproached them with their unbelief and hardness of heart, because they believed not them which had seen him after he was risen.

## upbraideth → reproaches — James 1:5
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: "reproaches" matches Geneva1599/YLT/Darby/UKJV exactly.
- NEW: If any of you lack wisdom, let him ask of God, that giveth to all men liberally, and reproaches not; and it shall be given him.

## upheld → lay — Isaiah 63:5
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "my fury, it lay me" is nonsensical. "Sustained" matches Geneva1599 exactly and is well-attested (Psalm 3:5, 55:22).
- NEW: And I looked, and there was none to help; and I wondered that there was none to uphold: therefore mine own arm brought salvation unto me; and my fury, it sustained me.

## upholden → rose — Job 4:4
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "have rose him" is ungrammatical (intransitive verb used transitively). "Raised up" matches YLT and is grammatically clean.
- NEW: Thy words have raised up him that was falling, and thou hast strengthened the feeble knees.

## upholden → rose — Proverbs 20:28
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "is rose by mercy" is ungrammatical. "Established" matches Geneva1599 exactly ("shalbe established with mercie").
- NEW: Mercy and truth preserve the king: and his throne is established by mercy.

## uprising → rising — Psalms 139:2
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: Geneva1599 attests exactly this simplification for both halves of the pair ("my sitting and my rising"), so dropping the "up-" prefix is a legitimate period-attested rendering, not mere mechanical trimming.
- NEW: Thou knowest my downsitting and mine rising, thou understandest my thought afar off.

## urge → quarrel — Luke 11:53
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "began to quarrel him" is ungrammatical (quarrel is normally intransitive). "Press" is KJV-attested in the identical construction at Luke 8:45 ("the multitude throng thee and press thee").
- NEW: And as he said these things unto them, the scribes and the Pharisees began to press him vehemently, and to provoke him to speak of many things:

## urgent → hold — Exodus 12:33
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "were hold upon the people" is ungrammatical. "Pressed" matches DRC's sense and is well-attested.
- NEW: And the Egyptians pressed upon the people, that they might send them out of the land in haste; for they said, We be all dead men.

## urgent → hold — Daniel 3:22
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "was hold" is ungrammatical. "Strait" is directly attested by Geneva1599 for this exact verse ("the Kings commandement was straite").
- NEW: Therefore because the king's commandment was strait, and the furnace exceeding hot, the flame of the fire slew those men that took up Shadrach, Meshach, and Abed-nego.
