# Batch 0107 proposals

## usurer → creditor — Exodus 22:25
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "an exact" is not a word; ASV/ACV render "a creditor," a common KJV-attested noun (2 Kings 4:1, Luke 7:41). Article changes an→a for the consonant-initial replacement.
- NEW: If thou lend money to any of my people that is poor by thee, thou shalt not be to him as a creditor, neither shalt thou lay upon him usury.

## usurp → have dominion over — I Timothy 2:12
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "to authority authority" duplicates a noun and drops the verb. ASV renders "have dominion over," a common KJV phrase (Genesis 1:26); DRC/Wycliffe confirm the "exercise power" sense.
- NEW: But I suffer not a woman to teach, nor to have dominion over the man, but to be in silence.

## vainglory → pride — Philippians 2:3
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "pride" is common in the KJV corpus, matches BBE, does not duplicate any word already in the verse, and does not invert the sense of self-exaltation.
- NEW: Let nothing be done through strife or pride; but in lowliness of mind let each esteem other better than themselves.

## vainly → proudly — Colossians 2:18
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "proudly puffed up" preserves the sense of unwarranted self-exaltation (compare BBE "foolishly lifted up"); common word, no duplication, no inversion.
- NEW: Let no man beguile you of your reward in a voluntary humility and worshipping of angels, intruding into those things which he hath not seen, proudly puffed up by his fleshly mind,

## variableness → changing — James 1:17
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "no changing, neither shadow of turning" preserves the parallel gerund structure of the original ("changing"/"turning"), a natural and common construction; meaning intact.
- NEW: Every good gift and every perfect gift is from above, and cometh down from the Father of lights, with whom is no changing, neither shadow of turning.

## variance → strife — Matthew 10:35
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "strife" is an extremely common KJV noun and preserves the sense of setting family members against one another; no duplication in this verse.
- NEW: For I am come to set a man at strife against his father, and the daughter against her mother, and the daughter in law against her mother in law.

## variance → debate — Galatians 5:20
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "strifes" duplicates "strife" later in the same list. Geneva1599 renders this word "debate" ("hatred, debate, emulations..."), a well-attested period synonym for contention that does not repeat another list item.
- NEW: Idolatry, witchcraft, hatred, debate, emulations, wrath, strife, seditions, heresies,

## vaunt → glory — Judges 7:2
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "beautify themselves against me" is semantically empty in context. Geneva1599 "make their vaunt against me" and DRC/Wycliffe "glory against me" show the sense is boastful self-exaltation; "glory" used as a verb is common in the KJV (e.g. 1 Corinthians 3:21).
- NEW: And the Lord said unto Gideon, The people that are with thee are too many for me to give the Midianites into their hands, lest Israel glory against me, saying, Mine own hand hath saved me.

## vaunteth → boasteth — I Corinthians 13:4

- NEW: Charity suffereth long, and is kind; charity envieth not; charity boasteth not itself, is not puffed up,

## vein → mine — Job 28:1
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "a going for the silver" is ungrammatical; ASV/BSB render "a mine for silver," which parallels "a place for gold" in the next clause and is common English.
- NEW: Surely there is a mine for the silver, and a place for gold where they fine it.

- NEW: Their wine is the poison of dragons, and the cruel gall of asps.

## vent → opened — Job 32:19
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "hath no open" is ungrammatical (adjective misused as noun). YLT renders "wine not opened," recasting the noun "vent" as a passive verb clause — a legitimate rephrase (rule 9) matching the sense of an unvented, ready-to-burst vessel.
- NEW: Behold, my belly is as wine which is not opened; it is ready to burst like new bottles.

## vigilant → temperate — I Timothy 3:2
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "husband of one wife, watch, sober" is ungrammatical (needs an adjective). "watchful" cannot be used — it is itself a flagged rare word elsewhere in this batch set (Revelation 3:2), which would create a circular rare-for-rare swap (rule 10). ASV/RWebster render "temperate," a common adjective that fits the list of qualities.
- NEW: A bishop then must be blameless, the husband of one wife, temperate, sober, of good behaviour, given to hospitality, apt to teach;

## vigilant → watch — I Peter 5:8
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "be watch" is ungrammatical. Geneva1599 and DRC both render this clause as an imperative verb, "Be sober, and watch" — a legitimate rephrase (adjective slot to verb clause, rule 8/9) with strong period attestation.
- NEW: Be sober, and watch; because your adversary the devil, as a roaring lion, walketh about, seeking whom he may devour:

## viler → meaner — Job 30:8
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "baser" shares its root with "base men" earlier in the same verse ("children of base men: they were baser than the earth"), a near-duplicate (rule 12). "meaner" is a distinct, common KJV word for the lowly/contemptible (compare "mean men," Isaiah 2:9; Proverbs 22:29) and avoids the repetition.
- NEW: They were children of fools, yea, children of base men: they were meaner than the earth.
