# Batch 0108 proposals

## villany → folly — Isaiah 32:6
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "vile" duplicates "the vile person" earlier in the same verse. Wycliffe/DRC/ASV consistently render this word "folly"/"foolish things," a common KJV word (Proverbs, Ecclesiastes) that avoids the repetition.
- NEW: For the vile person will speak folly, and his heart will work iniquity, to practise hypocrisy, and to utter error against the Lord, to make empty the soul of the hungry, and he will cause the drink of the thirsty to fail.

## villany → folly — Jeremiah 29:23
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "committed vile in Israel" misuses an adjective as a noun. Wycliffe/DRC/YLT/ASV all render this "folly" ("wrought folly in Israel"), a common KJV noun.
- NEW: Because they have committed folly in Israel, and have committed adultery with their neighbours’ wives, and have spoken lying words in my name, which I have not commanded them; even I know, and am a witness, saith the Lord.

## viol → psaltery — Isaiah 5:12
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Matches YLT "psaltery" exactly; "psaltery" is extremely common in the KJV corpus (Psalms); no duplication in this verse.
- NEW: And the harp, and the psaltery, the tabret, and pipe, and wine, are in their feasts: but they regard not the work of the Lord, neither consider the operation of his hands.

## viol → psaltery — Amos 6:5
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Matches YLT "psaltery" exactly; common KJV word.
- NEW: That chant to the sound of the psaltery, and invent to themselves instruments of musick, like David;

## violated → done violence to — Ezekiel 22:26
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "have imagined my law" is nonsensical and changes the meaning entirely. Darby/ASV/BSB render "done violence to my law," a common KJV construction ("do violence," e.g. Jeremiah 22:3) that preserves the sense of transgression.
- NEW: Her priests have done violence to my law, and have profaned mine holy things: they have put no difference between the holy and profane, neither have they shewed difference between the unclean and the clean, and have hid their eyes from my sabbaths, and I am profaned among them.

## viols → harps — Isaiah 14:11
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "the noise of thy flagons" is a meaning error — flagons are vessels, not instruments, and cannot make "noise" of music. "harps" is common, unambiguous, and not duplicated elsewhere in the verse.
- NEW: Thy pomp is brought down to the grave, and the noise of thy harps: the worm is spread under thee, and the worms cover thee.

## viols → harps — Amos 5:23
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "the melody of thy flagons" is the same category error as Isaiah 14:11. RWebster attests "harps" directly for this verse.
- NEW: Take thou away from me the noise of thy songs; for I will not hear the melody of thy harps.

## virtuously → worthily — Proverbs 31:29
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "valiantly" shifts the sense toward bravery rather than excellence of conduct. YLT/Darby/ASV all render "worthily," a common KJV word (e.g. "walk worthy") that better preserves the meaning of commendable behavior.
- NEW: Many daughters have done worthily, but thou excellest them all.

## visible → manifest — Colossians 1:16
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "manifest" is a very common KJV word meaning visible/apparent; pairs sensibly with "invisible" and is not duplicated elsewhere in the verse.
- NEW: For by him were all things created, that are in heaven, and that are in earth, manifest and invisible, whether they be thrones, or dominions, or principalities, or powers: all things were created by him, and for him:

## vocation → calling — Ephesians 4:1
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "call" is grammatically valid but far less KJV-common than "calling," which Geneva1599/Darby/ASV/ACV all use; "calling...called" is a deliberate cognate pairing attested by the witnesses, not a forbidden duplication.
- NEW: I therefore, the prisoner of the Lord, beseech you that ye walk worthy of the calling wherewith ye are called,

## volume → roll — Psalms 40:7
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Matches Geneva1599/ASV "roll" exactly; common word.
- NEW: Then said I, Lo, I come: in the roll of the book it is written of me,

## volume → roll — Hebrews 10:7
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Matches Darby/ASV "roll" exactly; common word.
- NEW: Then said I, Lo, I come (in the roll of the book it is written of me,) to do thy will, O God.

## voluntarily → freely — Ezekiel 46:12
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Matches Geneva1599 verbatim ("peace offrings freely vnto the Lord"); strong period attestation.
- NEW: Now when the prince shall prepare a voluntary burnt offering or peace offerings freely unto the Lord, one shall then open him the gate that looketh toward the east, and he shall prepare his burnt offering and his peace offerings, as he did on the sabbath day: then he shall go forth; and after his going forth one shall shut the gate.

## vowedst → didst vow — Genesis 31:13
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "where thou vow a vow" breaks the second-person past-tense inflection required by the surrounding "anointedst." RWebster's periphrastic "didst vow" restores correct grammar while replacing the flagged word.
- NEW: I am the God of Beth–el, where thou anointedst the pillar, and where thou didst vow a vow unto me: now arise, get thee out from this land, and return unto the land of thy kindred.

## voyage → course — Acts 27:10
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "course" is a common KJV word and preserves the sense of a sea journey; no inversion, no duplication.
- NEW: And said unto them, Sirs, I perceive that this course will be with hurt and much damage, not only of the lading and ship, but also of our lives.

## vulture → falcon — Leviticus 11:14
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "eagle" duplicates "the eagle" which opens this same list of unclean birds at verse 13. ASV/Darby render "falcon," avoiding the repetition while keeping a bird-of-prey sense.
- NEW: And the falcon, and the kite after his kind;

## vulture → falcon — Deuteronomy 14:13
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: Same duplication problem as Leviticus 11:14 — "eagle" opens this list at verse 12. ASV/Darby attest "falcon."
- NEW: And the glede, and the kite, and the falcon after his kind,

## vulture's → falcon's — Job 28:7
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR left "vulture's" unchanged, but the word is flagged and must go. ASV/ACV render "falcon's eye," a common, non-duplicating substitute.
- NEW: There is a path which no fowl knoweth, and which the falcon’s eye hath not seen:

## vultures → eagles — Isaiah 34:15
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "eagles" is common and not duplicated within this verse (unlike the Leviticus/Deuteronomy bird-lists, this verse has no earlier "eagle"); meaning intact.
- NEW: There shall the great owl make her nest, and lay, and hatch, and gather under her shadow: there shall the eagles also be gathered, every one with her mate.

## wagon → wain — Numbers 7:3
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "cart" is inconsistent with "wagons" used earlier in the same verse (the plural is not flagged and remains). Wycliffe attests "waynes" for this passage; "wain" is the same word-family in period English (cf. "hay wain") and avoids introducing a mismatched term.
- NEW: And they brought their offering before the Lord, six covered wagons, and twelve oxen; a wain for two of the princes, and for each one an ox: and they brought them before the tabernacle.

## wakened → stirred up — Joel 3:12
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "raised" risks confusion with resurrection imagery, a meaning shift from the intended sense of being roused to action. Darby "rouse themselves" and YLT "wake and come up" point to "stirred up," a common KJV phrase (e.g. many uses of "stir up").
- NEW: Let the heathen be stirred up, and come up to the valley of Jehoshaphat: for there will I sit to judge all the heathen round about.

## wakened → stirred — Zechariah 4:1
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "raised... out of his sleep" is an unnatural pairing (raised usually pairs with resurrection, not sleep). "Awakened" was avoided as a bare re-inflection of the flagged root; "stirred," in the sense used elsewhere in this same batch for related words, reads naturally with "out of his sleep."
- NEW: And the angel that talked with me came again, and waked me, as a man that is stirred out of his sleep,

## wakeneth → stirreth — Isaiah 50:4
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "stirreth" preserves the sense of rousing, is not duplicated, and reflects common KJV usage ("stir up").
- NEW: The Lord God hath given me the tongue of the learned, that I should know how to speak a word in season to him that is weary: he stirreth morning by morning, he stirreth mine ear to hear as the learned.

## walkedst → wentest — John 21:18
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's bare "walk" breaks the required -edst second-person inflection matching "girdedst." "wentest" is a very common KJV second-person past form (of "go," synonymous with "walked" in sense) that restores correct grammar.
- NEW: Verily, verily, I say unto thee, When thou wast young, thou girdedst thyself, and wentest whither thou wouldest: but when thou shalt be old, thou shalt stretch forth thy hands, and another shall gird thee, and carry thee whither thou wouldest not.
