# Batch 0111 proposals

## weightier → greater — Matthew 23:23
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "heavy" drops the comparative sense (more important, not merely "heavy"). "greater" is a common KJV word that correctly conveys the comparative "more important matters of the law."
- NEW: Woe unto you, scribes and Pharisees, hypocrites! for ye pay tithe of mint and anise and cummin, and have omitted the greater matters of the law, judgment, mercy, and faith: these ought ye to have done, and not to leave the other undone.

## weighty → grievous — Proverbs 27:3
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's second "heavy" duplicates "A stone is heavy" earlier in the same verse (rule 12). "grievous" is a common KJV word paired with burdens (Matthew 23:4, "heavy burdens and grievous to be borne") and avoids the repetition while keeping the sense of a hard-to-bear weight.
- NEW: A stone is heavy, and the sand grievous; but a fool’s wrath is heavier than them both.

## weighty → heavy — II Corinthians 10:10
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: "heavy and powerful" reasonably conveys forceful, serious letters; common word, no duplication, no inversion of meaning.
- NEW: For his letters, say they, are heavy and powerful; but his bodily presence is weak, and his speech contemptible.

## wellfavoured → beautiful — Nahum 3:4
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "loved" changes the nuance (loved ≠ attractive); Geneva1599/DRC both render "beautiful," matching the sense of an attractive harlot who lures nations.
- NEW: Because of the multitude of the whoredoms of the beautiful harlot, the mistress of witchcrafts, that selleth nations through her whoredoms, and families through her witchcrafts.

## wen → running sore — Leviticus 22:22
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: "having a have" is a blatant duplication error. RWebster attests "running sore," a plain, clear phrase preserving the sense of a blemish disqualifying an offering.
- NEW: Blind, or broken, or maimed, or having a running sore, or scurvy, or scabbed, ye shall not offer these unto the Lord, nor make an offering by fire of them upon the altar unto the Lord.

## wench → maidservant — II Samuel 17:17
- verdict: REVISE
- rationale: CUR's "handmaid" implies a personal servant of a specific master, which is not the sense here (a generic serving-girl carrying messages). Webster/RWebster/YLT render "maidservant," which is precise and common.
- NEW: Now Jonathan and Ahimaaz stayed by En–rogel; for they might not be seen to come into the city: and a maidservant went and told them; and they went and told king David.

## western → west — Numbers 34:6
- verdict: KEEP
- rationale: The repetition of "west border" at the start and end of the verse is itself attested in YLT ("As to the west border... this is to you the west border"), mirroring the Hebrew's own repetitive style; not a forbidden duplication but a faithful echo.
- NEW: And as for the west border, ye shall even have the great sea for a border: this shall be your west border.

- NEW: The wind goeth toward the south, and turneth about unto the north; it turneth about continually, and the wind returneth again according to his circuits.
