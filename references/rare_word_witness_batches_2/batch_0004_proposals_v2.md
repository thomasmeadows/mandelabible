# Batch 0004 proposals v2 (groups 121–160)

## everyone → every one — Matthew 7:26
- verdict: REPLACE
- suggestion: split into the two-word KJV-style form "every one" (both words common: "every" 1235, "one" 1976 occurrences).
- NEW: And every one that heareth these sayings of Mine, and doeth them not, shall be likened unto a foolish man, who built his house upon the sand:
- rationale: "everyone" as a single closed compound is a modern spelling; the period convention (and every witness above) is the two-word "every one." No self-certifying appeal to KJV needed — the words themselves are ordinary and abundant in the corpus.

## fearless → (see whitelist) — Genesis 1:26
- verdict: WHITELIST
- suggestion: best-effort common-word alternative would be "bold" or "unafraid," but this is not recommended — see whitelist-case.
- NEW: (no change recommended)
- whitelist-case: This verse's wording ("more numerous... rugged as the mountains, fearless and daring as the lion, alert as the fox, swift as the eagle") is recorded in the project's remembered-verses testimony as an owner-approved restoration, not an artifact of any translation corpus — none of the thirteen witnesses above contain this phrasing at all, because it is memory testimony, not a corpus reading. - rationale: Recommend WHITELIST. The word's rarity here is exactly what we'd expect from a memory-sourced restoration replacing a corrupted, more conventional verse — its absence from every witness is evidence of its testimonial origin, not of inauthenticity.
