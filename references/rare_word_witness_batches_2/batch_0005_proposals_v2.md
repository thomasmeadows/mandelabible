# Batch 0005 — Rare Word Proposals v2 (Round 2, revised rules)

## inquire → ask after — I Samuel 20:6
- verdict: REPLACE
- suggestion: "ask after" for "inquire after" — "ask" is attested 109×/"asked" 122× in KJV_restored; "inquire" appears only 1×.
- NEW: If thy father at all ask after me, then say, David earnestly asked leave of me that he might run to Beth-lehem his city: for there is a yearly sacrifice there for all the family.
- rationale: "Ask after" is common KJV-register idiom for solicitous inquiry and preserves the sense exactly. "Inquire" is the corpus's sole occurrence and has no local support beyond itself, so it fails the frequency test regardless of any pre-1611 attestation elsewhere.

## inscription → writing — Mark 15:26
- verdict: REPLACE
- suggestion: "writing" (41× in corpus) for "inscription" (only this occurrence).
- NEW: And the writing of his accusation was written above, THE KING OF THE JEWS.
- rationale: "Writing...was written" is mildly repetitive but grammatically sound and echoes constructions like "it is written" that recur throughout the corpus. "Title" (3×) was considered but is itself borderline rare; "writing" is solidly common.

No period-attested single word for "pedagogue/tutor" (schoolmaster, tutor, guardian) clears the frequency bar (all at 0).

## leach → leech (spelling) — Proverbs 30:15
- verdict: WHITELIST
- suggestion: best-effort failed candidate is "worm" (18×), but this loses the specific bloodsucking referent; no common corpus word names this creature.
- NEW: (no change recommended; if forced, "The worm hath two daughters, crying, Give, give...")
- whitelist-case: The word names a specific, distinct creature (the parasitic leech/horseleech of proverbial greed) exactly as almond, anvil, and ant were whitelisted in round one — no common corpus word (worm, serpent, viper) carries that referent without loss. - rationale: This is a true hapax naming one unambiguous creature; recommend WHITELIST (with optional spelling fix to "leech") rather than a lossy common-word swap.
